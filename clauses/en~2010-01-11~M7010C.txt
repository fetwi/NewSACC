 Use the following clause in standing offers when periodic usage reports are 
required from the offeror.

The standing offer authority must consult 8.75.1 of the Supply Manual for 
instructions on the reporting requirements.  The requirements must be detailed 
in an annex to the standing offer and must include the data to be reported on by 
the offeror.

The reporting periods and the number of calendar days by which each report must 
be submitted after the reporting period must be indicated in the clause.

The standing offer authority must ensure that the offeror fulfills all the 
reporting requirements detailed in the standing offer.
</section>
<h3 class="clause-heading">Legal text for SACC item</h3><pre><div class="sacc-item">
<p>The Offeror must compile and maintain records on its provision
of goods, services or both to the federal government under
contracts resulting from the Standing Offer. This data must include
all purchases paid for by a Government of Canada Acquisition
Card.</p>
<p>The Offeror must provide this data in accordance with the
reporting requirements detailed in Annex "<var>_____</var> ". If
some data is not available, the reason must be indicated. If no
goods or services are provided during a given period, the Offeror
must still provide a "NIL" report.</p>
<p>The data must be submitted on a <span class="input-required"><var>_____</var> <kbd>(<i>insert "quarterly basis" or
specify an alternate reporting period</i>)</kbd></span> to the Standing
Offer Authority.</p>
<p>(<em>If an alternate reporting period is required, delete the
quarterly periods provided below and define the alternate reporting
period</em>.)</p>
<p>The quarterly reporting periods are defined as follows:</p>
<p>1st quarter: April 1 to June 30;</p>
<p>2nd quarter: July 1 to September 30;</p>
<p>3rd quarter: October 1 to December 31;</p>
<p>4th quarter: January 1 to March 31.</p>
<p>The data must be submitted to the Standing Offer Authority no
later than <span class="input-required"><var>______</var>
<kbd>(<i>insert number of days</i>)</kbd></span> calendar days after the end
of the reporting period.</p>
