<pre>Use the following clause in any of the undernoted situations (refer to SPM 
Directive 6300):

	a) any single/sole source Request for Proposal for which a cost reimbursable 
method of payment is clearly indicated;

	b) any contract containing a cost reimbursable element within the basis of 
payment.

NOTES:  

	a) For printing requirements, use clause C0301D instead of C0300D.

	b) For Industry, Science and Technology Canada contracts providing assistance 
to contractors for the purchase of equipment, use clause C0302D instead of 
C0300D.

<h3 class="clause-heading">Legal text for SACC item</h3><pre>Upon completion of the Contract and also annually for multi-year contracts 
spanning more than one contractor fiscal year, the Contractor shall prepare 
and submit the Contractors Cost Submission, form DSS-MAS 7953, in 
triplicate to the Contracting Officer specified in the Contract. This cost 
submission covering each item with a Cost Reimbursable Basis of Payment in 
the Basis of Payment Section of the Contract shall be signed and certified 
by two officers of the corporation, including its chief financial officer 
(unless expressly provided otherwise in writing), and shall contain a 
breakdown of the following elements where applicable.

1)   Direct Materials

2)   Material Handling Overhead

3)   Direct Productive Labour by Cost Centre

4)   Manufacturing Overhead by Cost Centre

5)   Engineering Labour

6)   Engineering Overhead

7)   Direct Charges

8)   General and Administrative Overhead

9)   Other Contract Costs

10)  Profit

11)  Royalty

12)  Goods and Services Tax (only if the Contract is for an exempt supply)

13)  Freight Out

A single copy of reasonable supporting information for each cost element, 
   as considered appropriate by the Contractor, shall be forwarded at the 
   same time.  More detailed documentation as required under General (C) on 
   form DSS-MAS 7953 shall be kept readily available in the Contractor's 
   offices, in order that an in-depth audit can be carried out subsequently 
   as is deemed necessary.
</pre>
