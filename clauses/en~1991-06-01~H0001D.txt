<h3 class="clause-heading">Legal text for SACC item</h3><pre>1.     (a)  In this article, an amount is "due and payable" when it is due 
       and payable by Canada to the Contractor according to the terms  of 
       the Contract.

       (b)  For the purposes of this article, an amount is overdue when it 
       is unpaid on the first day following the day upon which it is due 
       and payable.

       (c)  In this article, "date of payment" means date of negotiable 
       instrument drawn by the Receiver General for Canada and given for 
       payment of an amount due and payable.

       (d)  In this article, "Bank Rate" means the discount rate of 
       interest set by the Bank of Canada.

2.     (a)  Canada shall be liable to pay, without demand from the 
       Contractor, simple interest at the Bank Rate plus 1 1/4 percent on 
       any amount which is overdue from the day such amount became overdue 
       until the day prior to the date of payment, inclusively; however, 
       interest will not be payable nor paid unless the amount has been 
       outstanding (unpaid) for more than fifteen (15) days following the 
       due date. Interest will not be payable on overdue advance payments. 

       (b)  The Bank Rate shall be that prevailing at the opening of 
       business on the date of payment.

3.   Interest shall only be paid when Canada is responsible for the delay 
   in paying the Contractor.  In the event Canada is not responsible for 
   the delay in paying the Contractor, no interest shall be paid. 
</pre>
