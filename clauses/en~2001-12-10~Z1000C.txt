<li value="2"><strong>Accompanying Documents:</strong>
<br/>As per the terms of the Contract, such an invoice must be
accompanied by documents as applicable below:
<br/><strong>Based on Shipping Terms:</strong>
<br/><strong><abbr title="Free on Board">FOB</abbr> Origin</strong>
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">If shipment is made by <abbr title="United States">U.S.</abbr> Government bill of

<br/><strong>N.B.</strong> Specific authority must be granted by the
 <abbr title="United States">U.S.</abbr> Defence Contract Management Americas (DCMA)
(Canada)/Procurement Agency to ship by commercial carriers, prepay
and add the freight charges as a separate item on the invoice.</li>
<li value="2">In all cases, invoices reflecting freight prepaid to
destination must be supported by an evidence of shipment (copy of
the commercial bill of lading) and should the charges be prepaid
and then added to the invoice in the same currency of the contract
and exceed $100 US, a receipted waybill must also be furnished to
substantiate the charge.</li>
<li value="3">Furthermore, if not specifically authorized in the
contract, a call to <abbr title="Defense Contract Management Americas">DCMA</abbr> (Canada) Transportation Division prior to

allow <abbr title="Defense Contract Management Americas">DCMA</abbr> (Canada) to advise the Contractor of the commercial
carrier to use to ensure proper government rates for the shipment
involved. This is done through their preparation of a <abbr title="United States">U.S.</abbr> DSA Form
359, Instructions to Contractors for Conus-Export Shipments. They
will issue this form for the Contractor's use. A copy of this form
must accompany the usual invoicing documents required.</li>
</ol>
<br/><strong><abbr title="Free on Board">FOB</abbr> Destination</strong>
<br/>All invoices must be supported by an evidence of shipment
(commercial bill of lading.)
<br/><strong>Based on Inspection and Acceptance Points:</strong>
<br/><strong>Inspection and Acceptance at Origin (Source)</strong>
<br/>Ensure that Block 21A of DD Form 250 is completed before
submitting it as your commercial invoice.
<br/><strong>Acceptance at Destination</strong>
<br/>In the event that acceptance of the supplies is to be
accomplished at destination, the invoices will not be paid pending
receipt by <abbr title="Canadian Commercial Corporation">CCC</abbr> of evidence of such acceptance, through the receipt
of a DD Form 250 signed at Destination Point (Block 21b.) or
through the receipt of payment from the customer.
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">Acceptance at destination - <strong>Inspection at Origin</strong>
(Source)
<ol type="1">
<li value="1">Ensure that Block 21a.of DD Form 250 is authorized for
inspection only.</li>
<li value="2">Ensure that 1 of the 4 copies of the DD Form 250 that
accompany the shipment is annotated "<strong>payment copy</strong>" - forward copy
to Block 12 address once signed to support payment in Block 23.
(Refer to <cite>Defense Federal Acquisition Regulation Supplement</cite>,
Appendix F-301 (12): Block 12: "payment will be made by" / <strong>code</strong>.
Enter the code and address of the payment office stated in the
Contract.)</li>
<li value="3">Request proof of delivery from the carrier and immediately
upon receipt forward same to <abbr title="Canadian Commercial Corporation">CCC</abbr>, Operations, referencing <abbr title="United States">U.S.</abbr>
Contract Number and applicable shipment number as reflected in
Block 2 of DD Form 250.</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li value="2">Acceptance at destination - <strong>Inspection at

<li value="3">Request proof of delivery (acknowledgement on the shipping

Block 2 of form DD Form 250.</li>
<li value="4">The Contractor will submit all required documents (although

</li>
</ol></li>

</ol>
