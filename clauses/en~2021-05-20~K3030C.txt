<p>
  Use the following clause in contracts if the client department has decided that 
the copyright in the work will belong to the contractor but the client wishes to 
obtain a license to exercise all rights comprised in the copyright in the work 
arising in the contractor's performance of the contract.
 </p>

<h3 class="clause-heading">Legal text for SACC item</h3><pre><div class="sacc-item">
<ol type="1">
<li value="1">In this section, <dfn>"Material"</dfn> means anything
that is created or developed by the Contractor as part of the Work
under the Contract, and in which copyright subsists.</li>
<li value="2">The Contractor grants to Canada a non-exclusive,
perpetual, irrevocable, world-wide, fully-paid and royalty-free
license to exercise all rights comprised in the copyright in the
Material, for any government purposes. Canada may use independent
contractors in the exercise of Canada's license pursuant to this
clause.</li>
<li value="3">Copyright in any translation of the Material made by
or for Canada belongs to Canada. Canada agrees to reproduce the
Contractor's copyright notice, if any, on all copies of the
Material, and to acknowledge the Contractor's title to the
copyright in the original Work on all copies of translations of the
Material effected by or for Canada.</li>
<li value="4">No restrictions other than those set out in this
section must apply to Canada's use of copies of the Material or of
translated versions of the Material.</li>
<li value="5">At the request of Canada, the Contractor must provide
to Canada, at the completion of the Work or at such other time as
Canada may require, a written permanent waiver of moral rights, in
a form acceptable to Canada, from every author that contributed to
the Material. If the Contractor is an author of the Material, the
Contractor permanently waives its moral rights in respect of the
Material.</li>
</ol>
</div></pre>
