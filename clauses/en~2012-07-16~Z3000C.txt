<h3 class="clause-heading">Remarks – Recommended Use of SACC Item</h3><section class="remarks group-sacc-remarks field-group-section">
<p>
  Use the following clause in contracts when Public Works and Government Services 
Canada (PWGSC) has authorized use of a priority rating.
 </p>
<p>
<abbr title="Public Works and Government Services Canada">
   PWGSC
  </abbr>
  is authorized to use the United States (U.S.) Defense Priorities and 
Allocations System (DPAS) regulation through annual applications to the
  <abbr title="United States">
   U.S.
  </abbr>
  Department of Commerce.  In operating under those authorities,
  <abbr title="Public Works and Government Services Canada">
   PWGSC
  </abbr>
  has a 
responsibility to monitor and report on the use of priority ratings.  Therefore, 
when the following clause is inserted in contracts dealing with Canadian 
Commercial Corporation contracts, the contracting officer is responsible to 
report its use by sending a copy of the contract preferably in
  <abbr title="Portable Document Format">
   PDF
  </abbr>
  format to the 
Defence Priorities and Allocations Officer, by e-mail to:
  <abbr title="Acquisition Branch">
   ACQB
  </abbr>
  Defence 
Priorities -
  <span lang="fr-CA">
<abbr title="Direction générale des approvisionnements">
    DGA
   </abbr>
   Priorités dedéfense
  </span>
  .  Alternatively, a copy can be faxed to 819-956-1459.
 </p>
<p>
  Note: A threshold of US$50,000 is included in the
  <abbr title="United States">
   U.S.
  </abbr>
<abbr title="Defense Priorities and Allocations System">
   DPAS
  </abbr>
  regulation (15
  <abbr title="Code of Federal Regulations">
   CFR
  </abbr>
  Part 700.17), where it is stated that for contracts under this value, a priority 
rating is not required "provided that delivery can be obtained in a timely 
fashion without the use of the priority rating."
 </p>
<p>
  Contracting officers must consult
  
   3.190
   
    U.S.
   
   Defence Priorities and Allocations System
  
  of the
  <cite>

    Supply Manual
   
</cite>
  for more 
information on the application of a priority rating.
 </p>
</section>
<h3 class="clause-heading">Legal text for SACC item</h3><pre><div class="sacc-item">
<ol type="1">
<li value="1">This is an urgent United States (U.S.) government
defence requirement and use of a <abbr title="United States">U.S.</abbr> priority rating is authorized
and assigned. This priority rating is valid only for the purchase
of materials, sub-assemblies and components (excluding controlled
goods) by the Contractor directly from <abbr title="United States">U.S.</abbr> contractors up to a
total of US$ <var>_____</var>. If the Contractor exceeds the
dollar limitation, the Contractor is authorized to proceed with the
purchase order while providing the Defence Priorities and
Allocations Officer, Public Works and Government Services Canada
(PWGSC), with details of the additional amount required, preferably by
e-mail to: 
ACQB Defence 
Priorities - DGA Priorités dedéfense.  Alternatively, a copy can be faxed to 819-956-1459.</li>
<li value="2">The assigned <abbr title="United States">U.S.</abbr> priority rating, required delivery
date, signature of an individual authorized to sign rated orders,
and the following certification must be placed on or attached to
the Contractor's purchase order to <abbr title="United States">U.S.</abbr> contractors:
<blockquote>"This is a rated order certified for national defense
use, and the Contractor is required to follow all the provisions of
the United States Defense Priorities and Allocations Systems
regulation (15 <abbr title="Code of Federal Regulations">CFR</abbr> Part 700)."</blockquote>
</li>
<li value="3">Assigned <abbr title="United States">U.S.</abbr> priority ratings may not be extended
within Canada. In consequence, when further materials are imported
through the Contractor's Canadian subcontractors or distributors,
the following paragraph must be added in the purchase order with
the Canadian subcontractor:
<blockquote>"<strong>PRIORITY ASSISTANCE</strong>: This is an urgent defence requirement. If the Contractor is importing any material/service
from United States (U.S.) for the fulfillment of the Contract,
contact the Defence Priorities and Allocations Officer, Public
Works and Government Services Canada , to request a <abbr title="United States">U.S.</abbr> priority
rating."</blockquote>
</li>
<li value="4">The Contractor must keep for a period of three (3)
years, and provide to Canada, on request, a record of its authority
to use the rating authorization and of all the uses made of it by
the Contractor.</li>
<li value="5">Special Priority Assistance:
<p>If the Contractor needs assistance in obtaining delivery under a
delinquent rated order with a <abbr title="United States">U.S.</abbr> contractor, the Contractor
should contact the Defence Priorities and Allocations Officer,
<abbr title="Public Works and Government Services Canada">PWGSC</abbr>.</p>
</li>
</ol>
</div></pre>
