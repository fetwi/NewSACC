<pre><div class="sacc-item">
<ol type="1">
<li value="1">Unless otherwise specified in the bid solicitation,
bids must be in Canadian currency.</li>
<li value="2">Bidders may request Canada to assume the risk for
exchange rate fluctuation. This request must be specifically made
at time of bidding.</li>
<li value="3">The foreign currency component is defined as the
element of the price that will be directly affected by exchange
rate fluctuations. It could include the net price <abbr title="Free on Board">FOB</abbr> foreign
manufacturer's plant, costs associated with applicable duty, excise
tax, Applicable Taxes, entry fees, transportation costs or delivery charges payable in a
foreign currency, and any other charges associated with being the
importer of record if they originated from and are required to be
paid in a foreign currency.</li>
<li value="4">The foreign value of the foreign currency component
of the bid or negotiated price must be provided <span class="input-required"><var>___________</var> <kbd>(<i>insert "in the bid" or
"before contract award"</i>)</kbd></span>. Form <a href="http://www.tpsgc-pwgsc.gc.ca/app-acq/forms/9411-eng.html" rel="external">PWGSC-TPSGC 9411</a>, Claim for Exchange Rate Adjustments, may be used for this
purpose. If milestone payments are proposed, it is recommended to
indicate on the above form the foreign currency component
associated with each milestone event.</li>
<li value="5">All bids are evaluated in Canadian currency.
Therefore, for evaluation purposes, the noon rate quoted by the
Bank of Canada as being in effect on date of bid closing, or such
other date as may be specified in the bid solicitation, will be
applied as the initial conversion factor for the specified
currency. (Column 3 of the above form will be completed by the
Contracting Authority.)</li>
<li value="6">Rates proposed by bidders will not be accepted for
the purposes of this exchange rate adjustment provision.</li>
<li value="7">If there are two (2) identical bids, and provided
that the bid selected would still be considered the most
advantageous to Canada, preference will be given to the Bidder who
assumes all or part of the exchange rate adjustment risk over a
bidder who does not assume any of this risk. Furthermore,
preference will be given to the Bidder who assumes all of the
exchange rate adjustment risk over a bidder who assumes only part
of this risk.</li>
<li value="8">Canada will pay the exchange rate adjustment amount
in Canadian currency using the prevailing noon rate on the date of
payment by Canada or, as applicable, in accordance with one of the
following clauses: <a href="https://buyandsell.gc.ca/policy-and-guidelines/standard-acquisition-clauses-and-conditions-manual/5/C/C3015C/active">C3015C</a>, <a href="https://buyandsell.gc.ca/policy-and-guidelines/standard-acquisition-clauses-and-conditions-manual/5/C/C3020C/active">C3020C</a>, <a href="https://buyandsell.gc.ca/policy-and-guidelines/standard-acquisition-clauses-and-conditions-manual/5/C/C3025C/active">C3025C</a>, or <a href="https://buyandsell.gc.ca/policy-and-guidelines/standard-acquisition-clauses-and-conditions-manual/5/C/C3030C/active">C3030C</a>.</li>
</ol>
