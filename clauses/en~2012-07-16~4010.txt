<h2 id="2012-07-164010copyright">4010 01 (2012-07-16) Copyright</h2>
<p>In this section, "<dfn>Material</dfn>" means anything that is created by the Contractor as part of the Work under the Contract, that is required by the Contract to be delivered to Canada and in which copyright subsists. "Material" does not include anything created by the Contractor before the date of the Contract. 
</p>
<p>Copyright in the Material belongs to Canada and the Contractor must include the copyright symbol and either of the following notice on the Material: ©  Her Majesty the Queen in right of Canada (year) or © <span lang="fr-CA">Sa Majesté la Reine du chef du Canada (année)</span>.
</p>
<p>The Contractor must not use, copy, divulge or publish any Material except as is necessary to perform the Contract.  The Contractor must execute any conveyance and other documents relating to copyright in the Material as Canada may require.
</p>
<p>The Contractor must provide at the request of Canada a written permanent waiver of moral rights, in a form acceptable to Canada, from every author that contributed to the Material.  If the Contractor is the author of the Material, the Contractor permanently waives its moral rights in the Material.
</p>
<h2 id="2012-07-164010translation-of-documentation">4010 02 (2012-07-16) Translation of Documentation</h2>
<p>The Contractor agrees that Canada may translate in the other official language any documentation delivered to Canada by the Contractor that does not belong to Canada under section 01.  The Contractor acknowledges that Canada owns the translation and that it is under no obligation to provide any translation to the Contractor.  Canada agrees that any translation must include any copyright notice and any proprietary right notice that was part of the original.  Canada acknowledges that the Contractor is not responsible for any technical errors or other problems that may arise as a result of the translation.
</p>
