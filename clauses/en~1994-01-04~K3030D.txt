<pre>Clause for contracts not involving research and development - the Contractor 
receives intellectual property.

Use the following clause in a contract where clause K3002D is used, if the 
client department requires the right to use, copy or translate material in which 
copyright subsists.
</pre>
</section>
<h3 class="clause-heading">Legal text for SACC item</h3><pre>1.     In this section, "Material" means anything that is created or 
       developed by the Contractor as part of the Work under the Contract, 
       and in which copyright subsists.

2.     The Contractor hereby grants to Canada a non-exclusive, irrevocable, 
       worldwide, fully-paid and royalty-free license to use, copy or 
       translate the Material for government purposes.

3.     Copyright in any translation of the Material made by Canada shall 
       vest in Canada, but this copyright shall be subservient to the 
       copyright in the original Material.

4.     Canada may use independent contractors in the exercise of Canada's 
       rights under this section.

5.     Canada shall reproduce the Contractor's copyright notice, if any, on 
       all copies or translations of the Material.

6.     No restrictions other than those set out in this section shall apply 
       to Canada's use of copied or translated versions of the Material.

</pre>
