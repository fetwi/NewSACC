<p>
  Use the following clause in contracts when progress payments are provided for on 
a fixed price contract.
 </p>
</section>
<h3 class="clause-heading">Legal text for SACC item</h3><pre><div class="sacc-item">
<ol type="1">
<li value="1">Progress payments will be made not more frequently
than once a month upon the following terms and conditions:
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">Progress Claims shall be completed in full, including
a brief report of the progress of the Work to the date of the
claim, and submitted to Canadian Commercial Corporation (CCC) on
form PWGSC-TPSGC 1111, Claim for Progress Payment.</li>
<li value="2">All the certificates appearing on the said form are
to be signed by or for the respective persons indicated
thereon.</li>
<li value="3">Payments will be made up to <var>____</var> percent
of the claimed amounts approved by <abbr title="Canadian Commercial Corporation">CCC</abbr>, but in no event will
cumulative payments exceed <var>____</var> percent of the Contract
value.</li>
<li value="4">Each claim will show:
<ol type="1">
<li value="1">expenditures during the claim period detailed in accordance
with the basis and/or method of payment terms of the Contract;<br/>
<strong>Note</strong>: Pro-rated profit not allowed.</li>
<li value="2">sales taxes (where applicable);</li>
<li value="3">Goods and Services Tax or Harmonized Sales Tax (where
applicable);</li>
<li value="4">holdback at <var>____</var> percent.<br/>
<strong>Note</strong>: Percentage factor to be shown on form
 <abbr title="Canadian Commercial Corporation">CCC</abbr> 747A.</li>
</ol>
</li>
</ol>
</li>
</ol>
<h2>Conditions Precedent to Payment</h2>
<ol type="1">
<li value="1">No payment shall be made to the Contractor, unless
and until:
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">invoices, inspection notes and other documents
prescribed by <abbr title="Canadian Commercial Corporation">CCC</abbr> are submitted in accordance with the terms of the
Contract or instructions of <abbr title="Canadian Commercial Corporation">CCC</abbr>;</li>
<li value="2">with respect to all materials, parts, work in process
or finished work, the cost of which has been paid by the Contractor
and in respect of which payment is being made by <abbr title="Canadian Commercial Corporation">CCC</abbr>, the
Contractor, if required to do so, establishes to the satisfaction
of <abbr title="Canadian Commercial Corporation">CCC</abbr> that the materials, parts, work in process or finished work
are free from all claims, liens, attachments, charges or
encumbrances;</li>
<li value="3">with respect to all materials, parts, work in process
or finished work, the costs of which have accrued in the accounts
of the Contractor as a liability to be discharged in the normal
course of business and in respect of which materials, parts, work
in process or finished work payment is being made by <abbr title="Canadian Commercial Corporation">CCC</abbr>, the
Contractor, if required to do so, establishes to the satisfaction
of <abbr title="Canadian Commercial Corporation">CCC</abbr> that the payment to be made by <abbr title="Canadian Commercial Corporation">CCC</abbr> shall be used only for
the purpose of discharging such liability and that upon such
discharge the materials, parts, work in process or finished work
shall be free from all claims, liens, charges or encumbrances;
and</li>
<li value="4">in the case of payment in respect of finished work,
such finished work has been inspected and accepted in accordance
with the terms of this Contract.</li>
</ol>
</li>
</ol>
<h2>Method of Payment</h2>
<ol type="1">
<li value="1">Payment by <abbr title="Canadian Commercial Corporation">CCC</abbr> to the Contractor for the Work shall
be made:
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">in the case of a progress payment other than the
final payment, within thirty (30) days following the date of
receipt of a duly completed progress claim, form PWGSC-TPSGC 1111;</li>
<li value="2">in the case of a final payment, within thirty (30)
days following the date of receipt of a duly completed final
progress claim, form PWGSC-TPSGC 1111, or within thirty (30) days
following the date on which the Work is accepted, whichever date is
the later;</li>
<li value="3">in the case of a final payment against a 'fixed-price
Contract', within thirty (30) days following the date of receipt of
a final invoice together with supporting documentation.</li>
</ol>
If <abbr title="Canadian Commercial Corporation">CCC</abbr> has any objection to the form of an invoice, within
fifteen (15) days of its receipt, <abbr title="Canadian Commercial Corporation">CCC</abbr> shall notify the Contractor
of the nature of the objection and payment shall be delayed until
thirty (30) days after the objection is resolved to the
satisfaction of <abbr title="Canadian Commercial Corporation">CCC</abbr>.
</li>
</ol>
<h2>Liquidation</h2>
<p>Except as provided in the Termination for Convenience clause,
all progress payments shall be liquidated by deducting from any
payment under this Contract, other than advance or progress
payments, the unliquidated progress payments, or <var>____</var>
percent of the amount invoiced, whichever is less. The Contractor
shall repay to <abbr title="Canadian Commercial Corporation">CCC</abbr> any amounts required by a retroactive price
reduction, after computing liquidations and payments on past
invoices at the reduced prices and adjusting the unliquidated
progress payments accordingly.</p>
<h2>Title</h2>
<ol type="1">
<li value="1">Title to the property described in this clause shall
vest in <abbr title="Canadian Commercial Corporation">CCC</abbr>. Vestiture shall be immediately upon the date of this
Contract, for property acquired or produced before that date.
Otherwise, vestiture shall occur when the property is or should
have been allowable or properly chargeable to this Contract.</li>
<li value="2"><dfn>"Property"</dfn> as used in this clause, includes all of
the items listed in subsections (a) through (d) below by the
Contractor that are or should be allowable or properly chargeable
to this Contract under sound and generally accepted accounting
principles and practices:
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">parts, materials, inventories, and work in
process;</li>
<li value="2">special tooling and special test equipment to which
the <abbr title="United States">U.S.</abbr> government is to acquire title under any other clause of
this Contract;</li>
<li value="3">nondurable (i.e. non-capital) tools, jigs, dies,
fixtures, mods, patterns, taps, gauges, test equipment, and other
similar manufacturing aids, title to which would not be obtained as
special tooling under subsection (b) above; and</li>
<li value="4">drawings and technical data, to the extent that the
Contractor or its subcontractors are required to deliver them to
 <abbr title="Canadian Commercial Corporation">CCC</abbr> by other clauses of this Contract.</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li value="3">The Contractor may sell any scrap resulting from
production under this Contract without <abbr title="Canadian Commercial Corporation">CCC</abbr>'s approval, but the
proceeds shall be credited against the Contract Price.</li>
<li value="4">To acquire for its own use or to dispose of property
to which title is vested in <abbr title="Canadian Commercial Corporation">CCC</abbr> under this clause, the Contractor
must obtain <abbr title="Canadian Commercial Corporation">CCC</abbr>'s advance approval of the action and the terms. The
Contractor shall
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">exclude the allowable costs of the property from the
costs of Contract performance; and</li>
<li value="2">repay to <abbr title="Canadian Commercial Corporation">CCC</abbr> any amount of unliquidated progress
payments allowable to the property. Repayment may be by cash or
credit memorandum.</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li value="5">When the Contractor completes all of the obligations
under this Contract, including liquidation of all progress
payments, title shall vest in the Contractor for all property (or
the proceeds thereof) not:
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">delivered to, and accepted by, <abbr title="Canadian Commercial Corporation">CCC</abbr> under this
Contract; or</li>
<li value="2">incorporated in supplies delivered to, and accepted
by, the <abbr title="United States">U.S.</abbr> government under this Contract and to which title is
vested in the <abbr title="United States">U.S.</abbr> government under this Contract.</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li value="6">The terms of this Contract concerning liability for
Government-furnished property ceases to apply to property to which
the Contractor has acquired title solely under this clause.</li>
</ol>
<h2>Risk of Loss</h2>
<p>Before delivery to and acceptance by <abbr title="Canadian Commercial Corporation">CCC</abbr>, the Contractor shall
bear the risk of loss of or damage to property, the title to which
vests in <abbr title="Canadian Commercial Corporation">CCC</abbr> under this Contract, except to the extent otherwise
provided in the Contract. The Contractor shall repay <abbr title="Canadian Commercial Corporation">CCC</abbr> an amount
equal to the unliquidated progress payments that are based on costs
allowable to property that is damaged, lost, stolen, or
destroyed.</p>
<h2>Control of Costs and Property</h2>
<p>The Contractor shall maintain an accounting system and controls
adequate for the proper administration of property.</p>
<h2>Reports and Access to Records</h2>
<p>The Contractor shall promptly furnish to <abbr title="Canadian Commercial Corporation">CCC</abbr> reports,
certificates, financial statements, and other pertinent information
reasonably requested by <abbr title="Canadian Commercial Corporation">CCC</abbr> for the administration of this clause.
 <abbr title="Canadian Commercial Corporation">CCC</abbr> shall have the right at any reasonable time to examine the
Contractor's records and accounts in regard to property.</p>
