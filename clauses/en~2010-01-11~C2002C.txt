<p>
  Use the following clause in all contracts with contractors located in 
California, when ownership will not be transferred to Canada until delivery of 
the goods, and when the contracting authority is to be informed of all duties 
and/or taxes that may or may not be required to be paid on the exported goods.
 </p>
<p>
  Use this clause in conjunction with
  
   D4003C
  
  .
 </p>
<p>
  Use this clause in conjunction with
  
   K9010C
  
  when the contract provides for 
progress or advance payments, or when the goods must remain in the State of 
California for a period of time.
 </p>
<p>
  For more information, consult
  
   Annex 4.3
  
  (12) and (13) the
  <cite>

    Supply Manual
   
</cite>
  .
 </p>
</section>
<h3 class="clause-heading">Legal text for SACC item</h3><pre><div class="sacc-item">
<p>The Contractor must inform the Contracting Authority of all
customs duties and of all sales, consumption, use, excise, personal
property or any other taxes the Contractor proposes to pay or not
to pay. The Contractor must carry out any instructions the
Contracting Authority may give in respect of payment or non-payment
of these taxes.</p>
</div></pre>
