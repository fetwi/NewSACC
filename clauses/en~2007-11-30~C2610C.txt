<p>
  Use the following clause in contracts for defence supplies when the Department 
of National Defence is the importer and the contract value is C$250,000 or more. 
 Contracting officers should request prices with customs duties identified as a 
separate item.  Clause
  
   D0035C
  
  may apply.
 </p>
</section>
<h3 class="clause-heading">Legal text for SACC item</h3><pre><div class="sacc-item">
<ol type="1">
<li value="1">As the goods to be supplied under the Contract are
defence supplies, customs duties on importation to Canada may be
remitted under the Tariff Item Number 9982.00.00 of the Schedule to
the <em>Customs Tariff</em>.</li>
<li value="2">Remission of customs duties payable may be granted
under the Tariff Item Number 9982.00.00 when the total contract
value of the defence supplies is C$250,000 or more. This reflects
the import value of the goods plus the duty that would be
applicable in the absence of the <em>Customs Tariff</em>.</li>
<li value="3">The Department of National Defence (DND) will be
responsible for prearranging remission on importation or for paying
customs duties on importation and applying to Canada Border
Services Agency for a refund. <abbr title="Department of National Defence">DND</abbr>  is also responsible for applying
to Public Works and Government Services Canada in good time for the
certification required by the <em>Customs Tariff</em>.</li>
</ol>
</div></pre>
