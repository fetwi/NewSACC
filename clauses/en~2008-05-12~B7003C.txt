 Use the following clause in contracts when government-supplied material will be 
used in the manufacture of items.  If a pre-production sample is not required, 
then contracting officers must delete paragraph 4 and renumber the paragraphs 
accordingly.
</section>
<h3 class="clause-heading">Legal text for SACC item</h3><pre><div class="sacc-item">
<ol type="1">
<li value="1">Government-supplied material (GSM) is the property of
the Government of Canada. The Contractor is responsible for
maintaining satisfactory records of the disposition of all
<abbr title="Government-supplied material">GSM</abbr>.</li>
<li value="2">The <abbr title="Government-supplied material">GSM</abbr> described in the Contract must be used in the
manufacture of the item(s) detailed in the Contract. Only the
quantity of material stated in the Contract will be supplied by
Canada without charge.</li>
<li value="3">If additional <abbr title="Government-supplied material">GSM</abbr> is required for contract
completion, it must be purchased from Canada at the unit price
stated in the Contract, Goods and Services Tax or Harmonized Sales
Tax extra. The Contractor must send the request for additional
material directly to the Contracting Authority at the address
specified on page 1 of the Contract. The request must be
accompanied by a certified cheque payable to the Receiver General
for Canada. The material will be shipped to the Contractor,
transportation charges collect.
<p class="description"><strong>Instruction to contracting
officers:</strong> Insert the following paragraph if a
pre-production sample is a requirement of the Contract. If a
pre-production sample is not a requirement then renumber the
paragraphs accordingly.</p>
</li>
<li value="4">The Contractor agrees that no <abbr title="Government-supplied material">GSM</abbr>, with the exception
of material required for the pre-production sample(s), will be cut,
used or processed until the Technical Authority has approved the
pre-production samples and provided a written notice of acceptance.
Damage incurred as a result of cutting <abbr title="Government-supplied material">GSM</abbr> before acceptance of any
pre-production sample(s) is the Contractor's responsibility.</li>
<li value="5">The Contractor must replace or make good, at its own
expense, any items which fail to conform to the Contract
requirements, as a result of faulty or inefficient cutting,
manufacture or workmanship.</li>
<li value="6">In the event of problems with the <abbr title="Government-supplied material">GSM</abbr> supplied, the
Contractor must advise the Contracting Authority immediately,
identifying the specific problem. If the Contractor proceeds
without guidance from the Contracting Authority, any costs incurred
and loss of <abbr title="Government-supplied material">GSM</abbr> will be at the Contractor's expense.</li>
<li value="7">The Contractor must rebate Canada for the value of
<abbr title="Government-supplied material">GSM</abbr> applicable to any quantity not delivered under the Contract.
The value will be calculated at the unit price and usage stated in
the Contract.</li>
<li value="8">Upon completion of the total contract quantity, if
there is <abbr title="Government-supplied material">GSM</abbr> with a total value in excess of $250, the Contractor
must either:
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">return the material to the sender with transportation
charges paid by Canada. The Contracting Authority must be contacted
to make appropriate arrangements; or</li>
<li value="2">apply for an overrun to the contract quantity.
Approval for an overrun will be provided under a contract
amendment.</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li value="9">Canada will not pay for work performed on any <abbr title="Government-supplied material">GSM</abbr>
that is damaged or lost while in the Contractor's care.</li>
<li value="10">The Contractor must not dispose of <abbr title="Government-supplied material">GSM</abbr> and/or
rejected units containing <abbr title="Government-supplied material">GSM</abbr> without the written approval of the
Contracting Authority. Transportation charges for the return of <abbr title="Government-supplied material">GSM</abbr>
where the Contract has not been satisfactorily completed will be at
the Contractor's expense.</li>
</ol>
</div></pre>
