<p>Suppliers who are subject to the Federal Contractors Program
(FCP) and have been declared ineligible contractors by Human
Resources and Skills Development Canada (HRSDC) are no longer
eligible to receive federal government contracts over the threshold
for solicitation of bids as set out in the <cite><a href="http://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/regulations/SOR-87-402/ " rel="external">Government Contracts Regulations</a></cite>. Suppliers may be declared ineligible contractors
either as a result of a finding of non-compliance by <abbr title="Human Resources and Skills Development Canada">HRSDC</abbr>, or
following their voluntary withdrawal from the <abbr title="Federal Contractors Program">FCP</abbr> for a reason
other than the reduction of their workforce to less than 100
employees. Any arrangements from ineligible contractors, including
an arrangement from a joint venture that has a member who is an
ineligible contractor, will be declared non-responsive.</p>
<p>The Supplier, or, if the Supplier is a joint venture the member
of the joint venture, certifies its status with the <abbr title="Federal Contractors Program">FCP</abbr>, as
follows:</p>
<p>The Supplier or the member of the joint venture</p>
<ol type="a">
<li value="1"><var title="checkbox">( )</var> is not subject to the
<abbr title="Federal Contractors Program">FCP</abbr>, having a workforce of less than 100 full-time or part-time
permanent employees, and/or temporary employees having worked 12
weeks or more in Canada;</li>
<li value="2"><var title="checkbox">( )</var> is not subject to the
<abbr title="Federal Contractors Program">FCP</abbr>, being a regulated employer under the <em><cite><a href="http://laws.justice.gc.ca/en/E-5.401/index.html" rel="external">Employment Equity
Act</a></cite></em>, <abbr title="Statutes of Canada">S.C.</abbr> 1995, <abbr title="chapter">c.</abbr> 44;</li>
<li value="3"><var title="checkbox">( )</var> is subject to the
requirements of the <abbr title="Federal Contractors Program">FCP</abbr>, having a workforce of 100 or more
full-time or part-time permanent employees, and/or temporary
employees having worked 12 weeks or more in Canada, but has not
previously obtained a certificate number from <abbr title="Human Resources and Skills Development Canada">HRSDC</abbr>, having not bid
on requirements of $200,000 or more;</li>
<li value="4"><var title="checkbox">( )</var> has not been declared
an ineligible contractor by <abbr title="Human Resources and Skills Development Canada">HRSDC</abbr>, and has a valid certificate
number as follows: <var>_______</var> .</li>
</ol>
