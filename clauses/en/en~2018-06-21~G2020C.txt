<h3 class="clause-heading">Remarks â€“ Recommended Use of SACC Item</h3><section class="remarks group-sacc-remarks field-group-section">
<p>
  Use the following clause in contracts when the contractor must use its own 
vehicle to perform the work even though automobile liability insurance, at 
varying limits, is statutory in all Canadian jurisdictions.
 </p>
<p>
  When contracting with rental companies for the use of short-term leases by 
Federal Government employees, refer to clause G6005C.
 </p>
<p>
  Contracting officers must choose options (e), (f), (g), (h), (i), (j), (k), (l), 
and/or (m) under paragraph 2 when applicable to their specific contract.
 </p>
<p>
  Contracting officers must consult
  <abbr title="Public Works and Government Services Canada">
   PWGSC
  </abbr>
  Risk Management Advisory Services at:
  
   NCR.RMIAS-SCGRA@pwgsc.gc.ca
  
  if the suggested limit of $2,000,000 per accident or occurrence is not considered adequate.
 </p>
</section>
<h3 class="clause-heading">Legal text for SACC item</h3><pre><div class="sacc-item">
<ol type="1">
<li value="1">The Contractor must obtain Automobile Liability Insurance, and maintain it in force throughout the duration of the Contract, in an amount usual for a contract of this nature, but for not less than $2,000,000 per accident or occurrence.</li>
<li value="2">The policy must include the following:
            <ol type="a">
<li value="1">Third Party Liability - $2,000,000 Minimum Limit per Accident or Occurrence</li>
<li value="2">Accident Benefits - all jurisdictional statutes</li>
<li value="3">Uninsured Motorist Protection</li>
<li value="4">Notice of Cancellation: The Contractor will provide the Contracting Authority thirty (30) days prior written notice of policy cancellation or any changes to the insurance policy.</li>
