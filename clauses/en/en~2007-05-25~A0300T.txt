<h3 class="clause-heading">Legal text for SACC item</h3><pre><div class="sacc-item">
<p>The following categories do not apply to standard and commercial
parts. Standard parts consist of common hardware parts and raw
materials, not necessarily designed for aviation use, produced to
recognized industry or government specifications, which are
available without proprietary limitations (such as Society of
Automotive Engineers (SAE), National Aerospace Standard (NAS),
Army-Navy Aeronautical Standard (AN), and Military Standard (MS)
hardware items). Commercial parts consist of common
non-aeronautical parts produced to recognized industry
specifications and available on the commercial market. Deliverable
standard and commercial parts must be in a new condition.</p>
<ol type="1">
<li value="1">Category #1 - New Materiel
<p>Deliverable end items to be manufactured or which have been
manufactured but not used, which are supplied by:</p>
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">the owner of the design or manufacturing rights to
the items; or,</li>
<li value="2">the authorized manufacturer or agent/distributor of
the owner of the design or manufacturing rights to the items;
or</li>
<li value="3">distributors approved by Transport Canada (TC) or
accredited by the Aviation Suppliers Association, for parts that
have an application to a civilian type certified aircraft; or</li>
<li value="4">maintenance organizations approved/accredited by <abbr title="Transport Canada">TC</abbr>,
the Department of National Defence (DND)/Canadian Forces Technical
Airworthiness Authority or repair stations certified by the Federal
Aviation Administration (FAA).</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li value="2">Category #2 - New Surplus Materiel
<p>Deliverable end items, unused and supplied by an entity other
than Category #1 sources. Full traceability documentation back to
the owner of the design or manufacturing rights to the items or
their authorized manufacturer or agent/distributor is required.</p>
</li>
<li value="3">Category #3 - Other Condition
<p>Any deliverable end item condition other than Category # 1 or
Category #2. Should the Bidder be offering deliverable end items in
Category #3, a complete description of the item's condition and all
available traceability documentation is required with the bid. Bids
containing parts identified in this category are subject to
evaluation by Canada.</p></li>
</ol>

<h2>Requirements for Airworthiness Certification</h2>
<p>The requirements for airworthiness certification do not apply to
the provision of standard and commercial parts. Standard and
commercial parts must be accompanied by a packing slip that
identifies the name and address of the supplier, the <abbr title="North Atlantic Treaty Organization">NATO</abbr> stock
number, identification of the manufacturing standard (e.g. <abbr title="Society of Automotive Engineers">SAE</abbr>,
 <abbr title="National Aerospace Standard">NAS</abbr>, <abbr title="Aeronautical Standard">AN</abbr>, MS) and/or manufacturer's part number and model number as
applicable; quantity, identification of the lot or batch number if
applicable; and the cure date/shelf life if applicable.</p>
<p>Bidders are advised that it will be a requirement of the
resulting Contract to provide with each item, supplied under the
resulting Contract, a Certificate of Conformance, or certified true
copies as specified herein:</p>
<ol type="1">
<li value="1">Category #1 and #2 military unique aviation
replacement parts must have an Original Equipment Manufacturer
(OEM) or an <abbr title="Original Equipment Manufacturer">OEM</abbr>'s approved manufacturer's Certificate of
Conformance, which includes all the following information:
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">positive identification of the item by type, class,
style, grade, model, part number, description, nomenclature and/or
serial number, as applicable;</li>
<li value="2">either the following certification, or a similarly
worded statement, signed by an authorized inspector, that satisfies
the intent of the following:
<blockquote>I certify that the aeronautical product described here
conforms to the applicable design data and is in a condition for
safe operations.</blockquote>
</li>
<li value="3">identification of both the authorized signatory and
the organization.</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li value="2">Category #1 and #2 items, which have an application
to a civilian type certified aircraft, must be supplied with a
Certificate of Conformance, namely:
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">form TCCA 24-0078, Authorized Release Certificate,
signed by a <abbr title="Transport Canada">TC</abbr> authorized inspector, within the two (2) years
before contract award;</li>
<li value="2"><abbr title="Federal Aviation Administration">FAA</abbr> Form 8130-3, Airworthiness Approval Tag, or a <abbr title="Federal Aviation Administration">FAA</abbr>
Form 8130-4, Export Certificate of Airworthiness, signed by a <abbr title="Federal Aviation Administration">FAA</abbr>
authorized inspector, within the two (2) years before contract
award;</li>
<li value="3">Joint Aviation Authorities (JAA) Form One, Authorised
Release Certificate, signed by a <abbr title="Joint Aviation Authorities">JAA</abbr> authorized inspector, within
the two (2) years before contract award;</li>
<li value="4">European Aviation Safety Agency (EASA) Form One,
Authorized Release Certificate, signed by an <abbr title="European Aviation Safety Agency">EASA</abbr> authorized
inspector, within the two (2) years before contract award; or</li>
<li value="5"><abbr title="Original Equipment Manufacturer">OEM</abbr>'s or <abbr title="Original Equipment Manufacturer">OEM</abbr>'s approved manufacturer's Certificate of
Conformance; which includes:
<ol type="i">
<li value="1">positive identification of the item by type, class
style, grade, model, part number, description, nomenclature, and/or
serial number, as applicable;</li>
<li value="2">either the following certification, or a similarly worded
statement, signed by an authorized inspector, that satisfies the
intent of the following:
<blockquote>"I certify that the aeronautical product described here conforms
to the applicable design data and is in a condition for safe
operations".</blockquote></li>
<li value="3">identification of both the authorized signatory and
organization.</li>
</ol>
</li>
</ol></li>
<li value="3">Bidders must specify which one of the documents
identified above will be provided for each item required to be
supplied in response to the bid solicitation.</li>
</ol>
