<h3 class="clause-heading">Remarks – Recommended Use of SACC Item</h3><section class="remarks group-sacc-remarks field-group-section">
<p>
  Include the following in solicitations that are subject to the Nunavut Directive.
 </p>
<p>
  For each new procurement that is subject to the Directive, an up-to-date supplementary template (Inuit Benefits Plan (IBP),
  <abbr title="Inuit Benefits Plan">
   IBP
  </abbr>
  Progress Report and
  <abbr title="Inuit Benefits Plan">
   IBP
  </abbr>
  Evaluation) must be obtained from Strategic Policy Sector’s Indigenous Procurement Policy Division (IPPD).
 </p>
<p>
  For important information on the implementation of Inuit and Nunavut benefits in procurements subject to the
  
   Nunavut Directive
  
  , please see the
  
   Guide on Government Contracts in the Nunavut Settlement Area
  
  .
 </p>
<p>
  For additional advice and guidance on the Nunavut Directive, the use of these clauses, and for the most up-to-date copy of the supplementary templates, please contact Strategic Policy Sector’s Indigenous Procurement Policy Division (IPPD) at:
  
   tpsgc.pacontratsnunavut-apnunavutcontracts.pwgsc@tpsgc-pwgsc.gc.ca
  
  .
 </p>
<p>
<strong>
   Note:
  </strong>
  These solicitation clauses cannot be used in conjunction with
  <abbr title="Standard Acquisition Clauses and Conditions">
   SACC
  </abbr>
  Manual items
  
   A3000C
  
  ,
  
   A3000T
  
  ,
  
   A3001T
  
  ,
  
   A3002T
  
  ,
  
   M3030T
  
  ,
  
   M9030T
  
  ,
  
   S3035T
  
  , or
  
   S3036T
  
  .
 </p>
</section>
<h3 class="clause-heading">Legal text for SACC item</h3><pre><ol type="a">
<li>Canada expects that the Contractor will, through the life of the contract, maintain and compile records as to the delivery of Inuit and Nunavut Benefits, including but not limited to the following elements: 
        <ol>
<li>Total hours and total dollars spent on Inuit Employment</li>
<li>Total hours and total dollars spent on Inuit Training</li>
<li>Total dollars spent on sub-contracting to firms on the Inuit Firm Registry</li>
<li>Location of Contractor and sub-contractors/suppliers in the Nunavut Settlement Area</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li>As part of the obligation under the General Conditions to keep proper accounts and records, the Contractor must maintain all records related to the delivery of Inuit and Nunavut Benefits and make them available for audit purposes. </li>
<li>Canada will expect that every invoice be accompanied by an <abbr title="Inuit Benefits Plan">IBP</abbr> Progress Report, in accordance with Annex “___” (<abbr title="Inuit Benefits Plan">IBP</abbr> Progress Report) of the Contract. </li>
<li>If, for any reason, a bid does not include an Inuit Benefits Plan (IBP), Canada will still expect that records documenting any unanticipated Inuit and Nunavut Benefits realized under the contract be provided with each invoice in accordance with paragraph c.</li>
