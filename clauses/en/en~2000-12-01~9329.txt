<strong>9329   01     (16/02/98)    Interpretation</strong>

1.     In the <em>Contract</em>,

       "<em>Canada", "Crown", or "Her Majesty</em>" means Her Majesty, the Queen in 
       right of Canada;

       "<em>Contract</em>" means these general conditions, any referenced provisions 
       and the agreement to which they are attached;

       "<em>Minister</em>" means the Minister of Public Works and Government 
       Services and any other person authorized to act on the <em>Minister's</em> 
       behalf.

       "<em>Work</em>" means the whole of the work, services, materials, matters and 
       things required to be done, furnished and/or performed by the 
       Contractor in order to carry out the <em>Contract</em>;


<strong>9329   02     (01/06/91)    Warranties</strong>

1.     The Contractor represents and warrants that the <em>Work</em> will be of a 
       proper quality, free from any defect in material and workmanship, 
       and shall be in full conformity with all other requirements of the <em>
       Contract</em>.

2.     Notwithstanding prior acceptance of the <em>Work</em> and without restricting 
       any other term of the <em>Contract</em> or any conditions, warranty or 
       provision implied or imposed by law, the Contractor, if requested by 
       the <em>Minister</em> to do so, shall replace or repair at its option and its 
       own expense any <em>Work</em> which becomes defective or which fails to 
       conform to the <em>Contract</em> requirements as a result of faulty or 
       inefficient manufacture, material or Workmanship, notice of which is 
       provided to the Contractor within a reasonable time during or after 
       the warranty period which commences the day after delivery and 
       acceptance of the <em>Work</em>. Unless otherwise stipulated in the <em>Contract</em>, 
       the warranty period will be 90 days or the length of the Contractor'
       s or manufacturer's standard warranty period, whichever is longer.


<strong>9329   03     (01/06/91)    Title, Acceptance and Risk</strong>

1.     Except as otherwise provided in the <em>Contract</em>, title to the <em>Work</em> or 
       any part thereof shall vest in <em>Canada</em> upon delivery to and 
       acceptance by <em>Canada</em> of the <em>Work</em> or any part thereof.

2.     Where title to any <em>Work</em> or any part thereof, or any rights vest in <em>
       Canada</em> under the <em>Contract</em>, the Contractor shall execute such 
       conveyances and other documents relating to title as may be required 
       to evidence the title or rights of <em>Canada</em>.

3.     Unless otherwise specified in the <em>Contract</em>, risk of loss or damage 
       to the <em>Work</em> or any part thereof shall remain with the Contractor 
       until delivery to and acceptance by <em>Canada</em>.


<strong>9329   04     (15/06/98)    Payment</strong>

1.     Payment by <em>Canada</em> for the <em>Work</em> shall be made following delivery, 
       inspection and acceptance of the <em>Work</em>, and following presentation of 
       invoices and such other documentation as the <em>Minister</em> may reasonably 
       require, and of which prior notice has been furnished.

2.     The period for payments shall be within thirty (30) days, calculated 
       from:

       (a)    the date the invoice is received, or
       (b)    the date of receipt of the goods,

       whichever is the latest date.


<strong>9329   05     (01/06/91)    Default and Bankruptcy</strong>

Without prejudice to any other rights or remedies, if the Contractor is in 
default in performance of the <em>Contract</em> or is bankrupt or insolvent or in 
receivership, the <em>Minister</em> may terminate all or a portion of the <em>Contract</em>. 

<strong>9329   06     (01/06/91)    Termination</strong>

1.     The <em>Minister</em> may at any time, by notice, terminate the <em>Contract</em> or 
       reduce the quantity or scope of the <em>Work</em> to be delivered and the 
       Contractor will give effect to such notice thereof according to its 
       terms.

2.     In such a case, all <em>Work</em> delivered and accepted and unpaid in 
       accordance with the notice shall be paid for in accordance with the 
       pricing basis established in the <em>Contract</em>.  The Contractor shall be 
       entitled to be reimbursed the actual costs reasonably and properly 
       incurred as a direct result of the termination, but in no case shall 
       such reimbursement exceed the <em>Contract</em> price or shall the Contractor 
       have claim for damages, compensation, loss of profit or otherwise, 
       except as herein provided.


<strong>9329   07     (01/06/94)    No Bribe, etc.</strong>

The Contractor represents and covenants that no bribe, gift or other 
inducement has been or will be paid, given, promised, or offered to any 
official or employee of <em>Canada</em> for, or with a view to, the obtaining of the <em>
Contract</em> by the Contractor.


<strong>9329   08     (01/06/91)    Members of the House of Commons</strong>

No member of the House of Commons shall be admitted to any share or part of 
the <em>Contract</em> or to any benefit to arise therefrom.


<strong>9329   09     (01/06/91)    Changes to Taxes and Duties</strong>

In the event of any change in any tax imposed under the <em>Excise Act</em>, the<em> 
Excise Tax Act</em>, or any duties imposed under the <em>Customs Tariff</em> or any other 
federal or provincial sales, excise or other like duties, taxes, charges or 
impositions after the bid submission date and which affects the costs to 
the Bidder/Contractor of the <em>Work</em>, the bid/<em>Contract</em> price shall be adjusted 
to reflect the increase or decrease in the cost to the Bidder/Contractor.


<strong>9329   10     (01/06/91)    Time of Essence</strong>

Time is of the essence of the <em>Contract</em>.


<strong>9329   11     (06/06/94)    Certification - Contingency Fees</strong>

1.     The Contractor certifies that it has not directly or indirectly paid 
       or agreed to pay and covenants that it will not directly or 
       indirectly pay a <em>contingency fee</em> for the solicitation, negotiation 
       or obtaining of this <em>Contract</em> to any person other than an employee 
       acting in the normal course of the <em>employee</em>'s duties.

2.     All accounts and records pertaining to payments of fees or other 
       compensation for the solicitation, obtaining or negotiation of the <em>
       Contract</em> shall be subject to the Accounts and Audit provisions of 
       the <em>Contract</em>.

3.     If the <em>Contract</em>or certifies falsely under this section or is in 
       default of the obligations contained therein, the <em>Minister</em> may 
       either terminate this <em>Contract</em> for default in accordance with the 
       termination for default provisions of the <em>Contract</em> or recover from 
       the Contractor by way of reduction to the Contract Price or 
       otherwise the full amount of the <em>contingency fee</em>.

4.     In this section:

       "<em>contingency fee</em>" means any payment or other compensation that is 
       contingent upon or is calculated upon the basis of a degree of 
       success in soliciting or obtaining a government contract or 
       negotiating the whole or any part of its terms;

       "<em>employee</em>" means a <em>person</em> with whom the Contractor has an employer/
       employee relationship; 
       "<em>person</em>" includes an individual or group of individuals, a 
       corporation, a partnership, an organization and an association and, 
       without restricting the generality of the foregoing, includes any 
       individual who is required to file a return with the registrar 
       pursuant to section 5 of the <em>Lobbyist Registration Act</em>, R.S. 1985 c.44 
       (4th Supplement) as the same may be amended from time to time.


<strong>9329   12     (01/12/00)    Interest on Overdue Accounts</strong>

1.     For the purposes of this section:

       "<em>Average Rate</em>" means the simple arithmetic mean of the Bank Rates in 
       effect at 4:00 p.m. Eastern Standard Time each day during the 
       calendar month which immediately precedes the calendar month in 
       which payment is made, where the "<em>Bank Rate</em>" means the rate of 
       interest established from time to time by the Bank of Canada as the 
       minimum rate at which the Bank of Canada makes short term advances 
       to members of the Canadian Payments Association.

       "<em>date of payment</em>" means the date of the negotiable instrument drawn 
       by the Receiver General for Canada and given for payment of an 
       amount <em>due and payable</em>;

       an amount is "<em>due and payable</em>" when it is due and payable by <em>Canada</em> 
       to the Contractor in accordance with the terms of the <em>Contract</em>; and

       an amount becomes "<em>overdue</em>" when it is unpaid on the first day 
       following the day upon which it is<em> due and payable</em>.

2.     Subject to the <em>Contract</em>, <em>Canada</em> shall be liable to pay to the 
       Contractor simple interest at the<em> Average Rate</em> plus 3 percent per 
       annum on any amount that is overdue, from the date such amount 
       becomes <em>overdue</em> until the day prior to the date of payment, 
       inclusive. Interest shall be paid without notice from the Contractor.

3.     <em>Canada</em> shall not be liable to pay interest in accordance with this 
       section if <em>Canada</em> is not responsible for the delay in paying the 
       Contractor.

4.     <em>Canada</em> shall not be liable to pay interest on <em>overdue</em> advance 
       payments.
