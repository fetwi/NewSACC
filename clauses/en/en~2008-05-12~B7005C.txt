<h3 class="clause-heading">Remarks – Recommended Use of SACC Item</h3><section class="remarks group-sacc-remarks field-group-section">
<p>
  Use the following clause in contracts when the fabric must be free from defects that may affect its appearance or serviceability.
 </p>
</section>
<h3 class="clause-heading">Legal text for SACC item</h3><pre><div class="sacc-item">
<ol type="1">
<li value="1">The fabric detailed in the Contract must be free from defects such as imperfections and blemishes that may adversely affect its appearance or serviceability when viewed under inspection conditions satisfactory to the Quality Assurance Representative (QAR) <span class="input-required"><var>_______</var> <kbd>(<em>insert the name of the
<abbr title="Quality Assurance Representative">QAR</abbr></em>)</kbd></span>.</li>
<li value="2">Any defect acceptable to the <abbr title="Quality Assurance Representative">QAR</abbr> must be strung (flagged) along the right hand selvedge of the face side using colourfast strings for each two (2) linear decimetres where the defect occurs (not applicable to narrow fabrics 15 <abbr title="centimetre">cm</abbr> or less in width).</li>
<li value="3">The Contractor must deduct allowances from the gross piece length for each defect or splice, on the basis of two (2) linear decimetres per fault. The Contractor must record gross
length, net length and number of splices on each piece ticket.</li>
<li value="4">Fabric with more than <span class="input-required"><var>_____</var> defects
<kbd>(<em>insert the number of defects allowed</em>)</kbd></span> per 100 metres <span class="input-required"><var>________</var><kbd>(<em>insert "square" or "linear"</em>)</kbd></span> will be rejected.</li>
<li value="5">The following defects, if prevalent throughout the fabric, will result in rejection of the full pieces: 
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">mill creases/calendar marks;</li>
<li value="2">edge to edge shading;</li>
<li value="3">tears, holes or marks beyond 12 <abbr title="millimetre">mm</abbr> from the outer edge of the selvedge;</li>
<li value="4">poor dye penetration and/or streaks;</li>
<li value="5">weak or tender fabric;</li>
<li value="6">warp or filling defects throughout.</li>
</ol>
</li>
</ol>
