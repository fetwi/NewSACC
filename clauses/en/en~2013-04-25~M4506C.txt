<p>
  Use the following clause in standing offers where there is a need to include a 
limitation of expenditure on the total value of the call ups.
 </p>
<p>
  If a limitation of expenditure is used in Part 7B. Resulting Contract Clauses of 
the 2T-RFSO1 template,  the fill in for the number of months before the expiry 
date of the standing offer must be consistent with clause
  
   C6001C
  
  , i.e. 4 months.
 </p>
</section>
<h3 class="clause-heading">Legal text for SACC item</h3><pre><div class="sacc-item">
<p>The total cost to Canada resulting from call ups against the
Standing Offer must not exceed the sum of $<span class="input-required"><var>_____</var> <kbd>(<em>Applicable Taxes excluded</em>)</kbd></span> unless otherwise
authorized in writing by the Standing Offer Authority. The Offeror
must not perform any work or services or supply any articles in
response to call ups which would cause the total cost to Canada to
exceed the said sum, unless an increase is so authorized.</p>
<p>The Offeror must notify the Standing Offer Authority as to the
adequacy of this sum when 75 percent of this amount has been
committed, or <var>_____</var> months before the expiry date of the
Standing Offer, whichever comes first. However, if at any time, the
Offeror considers that the said sum may be exceeded, the Offeror
must promptly notify the Standing Offer Authority.</p>
