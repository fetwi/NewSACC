<h3 class="clause-heading">Remarks â€“ Recommended Use of SACC Item</h3><section class="remarks group-sacc-remarks field-group-section">
<p>
  Use the following clause in contracts for the Department of National Defence 
(DND) when Special Production Tooling and Special Test Equipment (SPT/STE) is 
applicable.  This set of conditions pertains to cases where Canada expects the 
contractor to acquire or manufacture
  <abbr title="Special Production Tooling">
   SPT
  </abbr>
  and
  <abbr title="Special Test Equipment">
   STE
  </abbr>
  under the contract.  This 
materiel is not catalogued in
  <abbr title="Department of National Defence">
   DND
  </abbr>
  and becomes the property of Canada.
 </p>
<p>
  Contracting officers must indicate the name of the Technical Authority, 
Procurement Authority and Disposal, Sales, Artefacts and Loans (DSAL) Authority 
in the contract, as applicable.
 </p>
<p>
  Do not use this clause for Optimized Weapon Systems Management contracts.
 </p>
</section>
<h3 class="clause-heading">Legal text for SACC item</h3><pre><div class="sacc-item">
<ol type="1">
<li value="1">The Contractor must provide the Special Production
Tooling and Special Test Equipment (SPT/STE) to perform the Work
under the Contract as listed in Annex <var>_____</var>.</li>
<li value="2">The Contractor must identify each item of <abbr title="Special Production Tooling and Special Test Equipment">SPT/STE</abbr> as
Canada's property by affixing a plate, or by etching or by
stamping. The plate or marking must include reference to Public
Works and Government Services Canada (PWGSC) file number and
contract number, and a number for the <abbr title="Special Production Tooling and Special Test Equipment">SPT/STE</abbr> to identify the
individual item.</li>
<li value="3">The Contractor must provide an inventory in
electronic format on a Production Tooling Control (PTC) sheet for
each piece of <abbr title="Special Production Tooling and Special Test Equipment">SPT/STE</abbr> paid for by the Department of National
Defence. The control sheet must include:
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">a 5 <abbr title="multiplied by">X</abbr> 7 inch photograph of the <abbr title="Special Production Tooling and Special Test Equipment">SPT/STE</abbr> - the standard
of measurement (ruler) must be included in the photo;</li>
<li value="2">the Government Control Number which indicates the
item is government owned, relates to a specific piece of <abbr title="Special Production Tooling and Special Test Equipment">SPT/STE</abbr>
and sequential numbers;</li>
<li value="3">the contract number and date;</li>
<li value="4">the <abbr title="North Atlantic Treaty Organization">NATO</abbr> Stock Number and Part Number;</li>
<li value="5">a full description of the item;</li>
<li value="6">quantity;</li>
<li value="7">invoice price;</li>
<li value="8">reference number to the technical data sheet;</li>
<li value="9">original manufacturer;</li>
<li value="10">stock code and <abbr title="North Atlantic Treaty Organization">NATO</abbr> Stock Number and item name of
the product the <abbr title="Special Production Tooling and Special Test Equipment">SPT/STE</abbr> is used to produce.</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li value="4">The <abbr title="Production Tooling Control">PTC</abbr> sheet must be verified, signed and dated by
an authorized representative of the company. The Contractor must
provide the <abbr title="Production Tooling Control">PTC</abbr> sheet to the Contracting Authority who will forward
it to the <span class="input-required"><var>____________</var>
<kbd>(<em>insert "Technical" or "Procurement"</em>)</kbd></span> Authority,
and to the Disposal, Sales, Artefacts, &amp; Loans (DSAL)
Authority.</li>
<li value="5">Ownership of the <abbr title="Special Production Tooling and Special Test Equipment">SPT/STE</abbr> and any replacement belongs
to Canada and the <abbr title="Special Production Tooling and Special Test Equipment">SPT/STE</abbr> remains Canada's property at all
times.</li>
<li value="6">The Contractor must take reasonable and proper care
of the <abbr title="Special Production Tooling and Special Test Equipment">SPT/STE</abbr> while it is in its possession.</li>
<li value="7">The Contractor must provide written notice to the
<span class="input-required"><var>_____</var> <kbd>(<em>insert
"Technical" or "Procurement"</em>)</kbd></span> Authority at least sixty
(60) calendar days before the date when the <abbr title="Special Production Tooling and Special Test Equipment">SPT/STE</abbr> will no longer
be required for use in the performance of the Contract. The
Contractor must act as custodian of the <abbr title="Special Production Tooling and Special Test Equipment">SPT/STE</abbr> at no direct cost
to Canada. Disposal or redistribution instructions will be provided
by Canada when the notice is received or at the end of the
Contract. Preparation for the transport of dangerous goods,
packaging, and crating charges for redistribution of <abbr title="Special Production Tooling and Special Test Equipment">SPT/STE</abbr> must
be in accordance with the instructions provided by the Contracting
Authority.</li>
<li value="8">The Contractor must submit a separate invoice
accompanied by a <abbr title="Production Tooling Control">PTC</abbr> sheet. The Contractor must provide the
following information:
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">the Contractor's name;</li>
<li value="2">the date, and the <abbr title="Public Works and Government Services Canada">PWGSC</abbr> file number and contract
number;</li>
<li value="3">the supplies or components in the manufacture of
which the <abbr title="Special Production Tooling and Special Test Equipment">SPT/STE</abbr> is used;</li>
<li value="4">the quantity, the item number (reference paragraph 3
above), the production part number to which the <abbr title="Special Production Tooling and Special Test Equipment">SPT/STE</abbr> relates and
a brief description of the <abbr title="Special Production Tooling and Special Test Equipment">SPT/STE</abbr>; and,</li>
<li value="5">the price of each item of <abbr title="Special Production Tooling and Special Test Equipment">SPT/STE</abbr>, where available,
and the total amount.</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li value="9">The Contractor must forward the original and two (2)
copies of the invoice accompanied by the original and two (2)
copies of the <abbr title="Production Tooling Control">PTC</abbr> sheet to the Contracting Authority. The
Contractor must verify this inventory once every two years in
accordance with the <cite>Canadian Forces Supply Manual</cite> document
A-LM-007-014/AG-001.</li>
</ol>
