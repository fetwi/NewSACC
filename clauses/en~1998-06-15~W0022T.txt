<h3 class="clause-heading">Legal text for SACC item</h3><pre><div class="sacc-item">
<p>The benefits that apply to this procurement are contained in:
Section 2, Inuvialuit Final Agreement (IFA), clause:</p>
<ul class="hierarchy">
<li><span class="number">6.00</span> In accordance with normal
procurement practices, the contracting authority should develop
evaluation criteria to ensure fair consideration of all bids and
should consider all aspects of bidders' competencies and
capabilities. To avoid confusion, bid solicitation documents should
define qualitative terms or terminology critical to the contracting
situation.
<p>When establishing bid evaluation criteria for the awarding of
government contracts, and whenever practicable and consistent with
sound procurement management, contracting authorities should
consider the potential contribution of the Inuvialuit in carrying
out the contract. This may include, as appropriate:</p>
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">the employment of Inuvialuit, the engagement of
Inuvialuit professional services and the use of Inuvialuit
suppliers,</li>
<li value="2">the creation of administrative offices or other
facilities in the Inuvialuit Settlement Region,</li>
<li value="3">the undertaking of commitments, under the contract,
with respect to related on-the-job training or skills development
for Inuvialuit.</li>
</ol>
</li>
</ul>
