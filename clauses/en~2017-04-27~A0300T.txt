<h3 class="clause-heading">Legal text for SACC item</h3><pre><div class="sacc-item">
<p>The following categories do not apply to standard and commercial parts. Standard parts consist of common hardware parts and raw materials, not necessarily designed for aviation use, produced to recognized industry or government specifications, which are available without proprietary limitations (such as Society of
Automotive Engineers (SAE), National Aerospace Standard (NAS), Army-Navy Aeronautical Standard (AN), and Military Standard (MS) hardware items). Commercial parts consist of common non-aeronautical parts produced to recognized industry specifications and available on the commercial market. Deliverable standard and commercial parts must be in a new condition.</p>
<ol type="1">
<li value="1">Category #1 - New Materiel<br/>
Deliverable end items to be manufactured or which have been manufactured but not used, which are supplied by:
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">the owner of the design or manufacturing rights to the items; or,</li>
<li value="2">the authorized manufacturer or agent/distributor of the owner of the design or manufacturing rights to the items; or</li>
<li value="3">distributors approved by Transport Canada (TC) or accredited by the Aviation Suppliers Association, for parts that have an application to a civilian type certified aircraft; or</li>
<li value="4">maintenance organizations approved/accredited by <abbr title="Transport Canada">TC</abbr>, the Department of National Defence (DND)/Canadian Forces Technical Airworthiness Authority or repair stations certified by the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA).</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li value="2">Category #2 - New Surplus Materiel<br/>
Deliverable end items, unused and supplied by an entity other than Category #1 sources. Full traceability documentation back to the owner of the design or manufacturing rights to the items or their authorized manufacturer or agent/distributor is required. 
</li>
<li value="3">Category #3 - Other Condition<br/>
Any deliverable end item condition other than Category # 1 or Category #2. Should the Bidder be offering deliverable end items in Category #3, a complete description of the item's condition and all available traceability documentation is required with the bid. Bids containing parts identified in this category are subject to evaluation by Canada.</li>
</ol>

<h2>Requirements for Airworthiness Certification</h2>
<p>The requirements for airworthiness certification do not apply to the provision of standard and commercial parts. Standard and commercial parts must be accompanied by a packing slip that identifies the name and address of the supplier, the <abbr title="North Atlantic Treaty Organization">NATO</abbr> stock number, identification of the manufacturing standard (e.g. <abbr title="Society of Automotive Engineers">SAE</abbr>, <abbr title="National Aerospace Standard">NAS</abbr>, <abbr title="Aeronautical Standard">AN</abbr>, <abbr title="Military Standard">MS</abbr>) and manufacturer's part number and model number as applicable; quantity, identification of the lot or batch number if applicable; and the cure date/shelf life if applicable.</p>
<p>Bidders are advised that the Contractor must provide the following airworthiness documentation, for each unit of issue, within the interior packaging or attached to the good(s) supplied under the resulting contract. Note that this requirement is in addition to documentation required in support of invoice payment or other documentation requirements identified within the Contract:</p>
<ol type="1">
<li value="1">Category #1 and #2 military unique aviation replacement parts must have an Original Equipment Manufacturer (OEM) or an <abbr title="Original Equipment Manufacturer">OEM</abbr>'s approved manufacturer's Certificate of Conformance, which includes all the following information:
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">positive identification of the item by type, class, style, grade, model, part number, description, nomenclature and/or serial number, as applicable;</li>
<li value="2">either the following certification, or a similarly worded statement, signed by an authorized inspector, that satisfies the intent of the following:
<blockquote>I certify that the aeronautical product described here conforms to the applicable design data and is in a condition for safe operations.</blockquote>
</li>
<li value="3">identification of both the authorized signatory and the organization.</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li value="2">Category #1 and #2 items, which have an application to a civilian type certified aircraft, must be supplied with a Certificate of Conformance, namely:
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">form <abbr title="Transport Canada Civil Aviation">TCCA</abbr> Form One, Authorized Release Certificate, signed by a <abbr title="Transport Canada">TC</abbr> authorized inspector, within the two (2) years before contract award;</li>
<li value="2"><abbr title="Federal Aviation Administration">FAA</abbr> Form 8130-3, Airworthiness Approval Tag, or a <abbr title="Federal Aviation Administration">FAA</abbr> Form 8130-4, Export Certificate of Airworthiness, signed by a <abbr title="Federal Aviation Administration">FAA</abbr> authorized inspector, within the two (2) years before contract award;</li>
<li value="3">European Aviation Safety Agency (EASA) Form One, Authorized Release Certificate, signed by an <abbr title="European Aviation Safety Agency">EASA</abbr> authorized inspector, within the two (2) years before contract award; or</li>
<li value="4"><abbr title="Original Equipment Manufacturer">OEM</abbr>'s or <abbr title="Original Equipment Manufacturer">OEM</abbr>'s approved manufacturer's Certificate of Conformance; which includes:
<ol type="i">
<li value="1">positive identification of the item by type, class style, grade, model, part number, description, nomenclature, and/or serial number, as applicable;</li>
<li value="2">either the following certification, or a similarly worded statement, signed by an authorized inspector, that satisfies the intent of the following:
<blockquote>I certify that the aeronautical product described here conforms to the applicable design data and is in a condition for safe operations.</blockquote></li>
<li value="3">identification of both the authorized signatory and organization.</li>
</ol>
</li>
</ol></li>
<li value="3">Bidders must specify which one of the documents identified above will be provided for each item required to be supplied in response to the bid solicitation.</li>
</ol>
