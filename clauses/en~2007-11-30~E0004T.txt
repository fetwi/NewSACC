<pre>1.     A bid financial security is required and must consist of:

       (a)    a security deposit as defined below, or

       (b)    a bid bond, form PWGSC-TPSGC 504 attached <a href="http://www.pwgsc.gc.ca/acquisitions/text/forms/forms-e.html" rel="external">http://www.pwgsc.gc.ca
              /acquisitions/text/forms/forms-e.html</a> to be accepted as 
              security by one of the insurance companies listed in Treasury 
              Board Contracting Policy, Appendix L, Acceptable Bonding 
              Companies <a href="http://www.tbs-sct.gc.ca/pubs_pol/dcgpubs/contracting/contractingpol_l_e.asp" rel="external">http://www.tbs-sct.gc.ca/pubs_pol/dcgpubs/
              contracting/contractingpol_l_e.asp</a>. 

2.     Security deposits in the form of coupon bonds must be accompanied by 
       all coupons that are unmatured at the time such bonds are delivered 
       to Canada.  Canada may, from time to time detach matured coupons and 
       send them to the Bidder at the address noted on the bid, unless 
       specified otherwise by the Contractor.

3.     Where the bid price is $250,000 or less, the security deposit will 
       be _____ percent.  Where the bid price exceeds $250,000, the 
       security deposit will be $_____ plus _____ percent of the amount by 
       which the bid price exceeds $250,000, to a maximum of $_____. 

4.     Bidders who supply a security deposit as bid security are required 
       to submit their bids under seal (does not apply in Quebec).
