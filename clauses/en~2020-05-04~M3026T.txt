<h3 class="clause-heading">Legal text for SACC item</h3><pre><div class="sacc-item">
<p>Contracts awarded to former public servants (FPS) in receipt of a pension or of a lump sum payment must bear the closest public scrutiny and reflect fairness in the spending of public funds. In order to comply with Treasury Board policies and directives on contracts awarded to FPS, offerors must provide the information required below before the issuance of a standing offer.  If the answer to the questions and, as applicable the information required have not been received by the time the evaluation of offers is completed, Canada will inform the Offeror of a time frame within which to provide the information.  Failure to comply with Canada’s request and meet the requirement within the prescribed time frame will render the offer non-responsive.</p>
<h2>Definitions</h2>
<p>For the purposes of this clause,</p>
<p>"<q>former public servant</q>" is any former member of a department as
defined in the <cite>Financial Administration Act</cite>, <abbr title="Revised Statutes">R.S.</abbr>,
1985, <abbr title="chapter">c.</abbr> F-11, a former member of the Canadian Armed Forces or a
former member of the Royal Canadian Mounted Police. A former public
servant may be:</p>
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">an individual;</li>
<li value="2">an individual who has incorporated;</li>
<li value="3">a partnership made of former public servants; or</li>
<li value="4">a sole proprietorship or entity where the affected
individual has a controlling or major interest in the entity.</li>
</ol>
<p><dfn>"<q>lump sum payment period</q>"</dfn> means the period measured in
weeks of salary, for which payment has been made to facilitate the
transition to retirement or to other employment as a result of the
implementation of various programs to reduce the size of the Public
Service. The lump sum payment period does not include the period of
severance pay, which is measured in a like manner.</p>
<p><dfn>"<q>pension</q>"</dfn> means a pension or annual allowance paid under the <cite>Public Service Superannuation Act</cite> (PSSA), <abbr title="Revised Statutes">R.S.</abbr>, 1985, <abbr title="chapter">c.</abbr> P-36, and
any increases paid pursuant to the <cite>Supplementary Retirement Benefits Act</cite>, <abbr title="Revised Statutes">R.S.</abbr>, 1985, <abbr title="chapter">c.</abbr> S-24 as it affects the <abbr title="Public Service Superannuation Act">PSSA</abbr>. It
does not include pensions payable pursuant to the <cite>Canadian Forces Superannuation Act</cite>, <abbr title="Revised Statutes">R.S.</abbr>, 1985, <abbr title="chapter">c.</abbr> C-17, the
<cite>Defence Services Pension Continuation Act</cite>, 1970, <abbr title="chapter">c.</abbr> D-3, the <cite>Royal Canadian Mounted Police Pension Continuation Act</cite>, 1970, <abbr title="chapter">c.</abbr> R-10 and the <cite>Royal Canadian Mounted Police Superannuation Act</cite>, <abbr title="Revised Statutes">R.S.</abbr>, 1985, <abbr title="chapter">c.</abbr> R-11, the
<cite>Members of Parliament Retiring Allowances Act</cite>, <abbr title="Revised Statutes">R.S.</abbr>, 1985,
<abbr title="chapter">c.</abbr> M-5, and that portion of pension payable to the <cite>Canada Pension Plan Act</cite>, <abbr title="Revised Statutes">R.S.</abbr> 1985, <abbr title="chapter">c.</abbr> C-8.</p>
<h2>Former Public Servant in Receipt of a Pension</h2>
<p>As per the above definitions, is the Offeror a <abbr title="Former public servant">FPS</abbr> in receipt of a pension?
<strong>YES</strong> <var title="checkbox">( )</var>
<strong>NO</strong> <var title="checkbox">( )</var></p>
<p>If so, the Offeror must provide the following information, for all <abbr title="Former public servant">FPS</abbr> in receipt of a pension, as applicable:</p>
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">name of former public servant;</li>
<li value="2">date of termination of employment or retirement from
the Public Service.</li>
</ol>
<p>By providing this information, Offerors agree that the successful Offeror’s status, with respect to being a former public servant in receipt of a pension, will be reported on departmental websites as part of the published proactive disclosure reports in accordance with Contracting Policy Notice: 2019-01 and the Guidelines on the Proactive Disclosure of Contracts.</p>

the terms of the Work Force Adjustment Directive? <strong>YES</strong>

<p>If so, the Offeror must provide the following information:</p>
<ol type="a">
