<pre><div class="sacc-item">
<ol type="1">
<li value="1"><dfn>"security deposit"</dfn> means
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">a bill of exchange that is payable to the Receiver General for Canada and certified by an approved financial institution or drawn by an approved financial institution on itself; or</li>
<li value="2">a government guaranteed bond; or</li>
<li value="3">an irrevocable standby letter of credit, or</li>
<li value="4">such other security as may be considered appropriate by the Contracting Authority and approved by Treasury Board;</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li value="2"><dfn>"approved financial institution"</dfn> means 
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">any corporation or institution that is a member of the Canadian Payments Association (Payments Canada);</li>
<li value="2">a corporation that accepts deposits that are insured by the Canada Deposit Insurance Corporation or the <span lang="fr-CA">"Régie de
l'assurance-dépôts du Québec"</span> to the maximum permitted by law;</li>
<li value="3">a credit union as defined in paragraph 137(6) of the <a href="http://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/acts/I-3.3/index.html" rel="external">Income Tax Act</a>;</li>
<li value="4">a corporation that accepts deposits from the public, if repayment of the deposits is guaranteed by a Canadian province or territory; or</li>
<li value="5">the Canada Post Corporation.</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li value="3"><dfn>"government guaranteed bond"</dfn> means a bond of the Government of Canada or a bond unconditionally guaranteed as to principal and interest by the Government of Canada that is:
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">payable to bearer;</li>
<li value="2">accompanied by a duly executed instrument of transfer of the bond to the Receiver General for Canada in accordance with the <a href="http://laws.justice.gc.ca/eng/regulations/C.R.C.,_c._698/page-1.html" rel="external">Domestic Bonds of Canada Regulations</a>;</li>
<li value="3">registered in the name of the Receiver General for Canada.</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li value="4"><dfn>"irrevocable standby letter of credit"</dfn>
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">means any arrangement, however named or described, whereby a financial institution (the "Issuer"), acting at the request and on the instructions of a customer (the "Applicant"), or on its behalf,
<ol type="i">
<li value="1">will make a payment to or to the order of Canada, as the beneficiary;</li>
<li value="2">will accept and pay bills of exchange drawn by Canada;</li>
<li value="3">authorizes another financial institution to effect such payment, or accept and pay such bills of exchange; or</li>
<li value="4">authorizes another financial institution to negotiate, against written demand(s) for payment, provided that the conditions of the letter of credit are complied with.</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li value="2">must state the face amount which may be drawn against it;</li>
<li value="3">must state its expiry date;</li>
<li value="4">must provide for sight payment to the Receiver General for Canada by way of the financial institution's draft against presentation of a written demand for payment signed by the authorized departmental representative identified in the letter of credit by his or her office;</li>
<li value="5">must provide that more than one written demand for payment may be presented subject to the sum of those demands not exceeding the face amount of the letter of credit;</li>
<li value="6">must provide that it is subject to the International Chamber of Commerce (ICC) Uniform Customs and Practice (UCP) for Documentary Credits, 2007 Revision, <abbr title="International Chamber of Commerce">ICC</abbr> Publication <abbr title="Number">No.</abbr> 600. Pursuant to the <abbr title="International Chamber of Commerce">ICC</abbr> <abbr title="Uniform Customs and Practice">UCP</abbr>, a credit is irrevocable even if there is no indication to that effect; and</li>
<li value="7">must be issued (Issuer) or confirmed (Confirmer), in either official language, by a financial institution that is a member of the Canadian Payments Association (Payments Canada) and is on the letterhead of the Issuer or Confirmer. The format is left to the discretion of the Issuer or Confirmer.</li>
</ol>
</li></ol>
</div></pre>
