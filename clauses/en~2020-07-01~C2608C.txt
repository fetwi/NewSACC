<h2>General</h2>
<ol type="1">
<li value="1">The Contractor must provide two (2) copies of the Canada Customs Invoice (CCI) or two (2) copies of the commercial invoice marked "For Customs Purposes Only".</li>
<li value="2">Shipments from the United States and Mexico that are of American, Mexican or Canadian origin, as defined by the <cite>Canada-United States-Mexico Agreement</cite> (CUSMA):
            <ol type="a">
<li>For goods valued at more than USD$1,000, the Contractor must provide a certification of origin of the goods that demonstrate that the good is originating. This may be provided on the commercial invoice or any other document and does not need to follow a prescribed format, but must:
                    <ol type="i">
<li>Describe the originating good in sufficient detail to enable its identification;</li>
<li>Meet the requirements as set out in the <a href="https://ustr.gov/trade-agreements/free-trade-agreements/united-states-mexico-canada-agreement/uniform-regulations" rel="external">Uniform Regulations for Rules of Origin</a>; and</li>
<li>Contain a set of minimum data elements as described in Annex 5-A (<a href="https://www.international.gc.ca/trade-commerce/assets/pdfs/agreements-accords/cusma-aceum/r-cusma-05.pdf" rel="external">Chapter 5</a> of <abbr title="Canada-United States-Mexico Agreement">CUSMA</abbr>) that indicate that the good is both originating and meets the requirements of Chapter 5.</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li>For goods valued at USD$1,000 or less, the proof must be a statement on the invoice certifying that the good qualifies as an originating good.
                    </li>
</ol>
In either case, an original signature and a reference to the contract number must be included in the document. For contracts valued at C$250,000 or more, the certification of origin will not be required. If applicable, the exchange rate to be used to determine the value of goods in USD is the exchange rate on the Direct Shipment Date, which is the date the shipment starts its direct journey to the Buyer.
        </li>
<li value="3">For shipments from Israel that are Israeli in origin, as defined by the Canada-Israel Free Trade Agreement (CIFTA), the Contractor must provide the certification of origin of the goods. This proof must be in the form of a <abbr title="Canada-Israel Free Trade Agreement">CIFTA</abbr> Certificate of Origin for goods valued at more than C$1,600, or for goods valued at C$1,600 or less, a statement on the invoice certifying that the good qualifies as an originating good. In either case, an original signature and a reference to the contract number must be included in the document. For contracts valued at C$250,000 or more, the proof of origin will not be required.</li>
<li value="4">The Contractor must not employ commercial customs brokers to custom clear the goods provided under the Contract, unless authorized by the Canadian Material Support Group / Customs, at National Defence Headquarters, telephone: 1-855-210-5149, facsimile: 1-800-306-1811 or 613-971-7333.</li>
</ol>
<h2>Completion of Documents</h2>
<p>The <abbr title="Canada Customs Invoice">CCI</abbr> or commercial invoice must include the following information:</p>
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">complete description of the goods being shipped,
            including the applicable United States <cite>"<a href="http://www.census.gov/foreign-trade/schedules/b/index.html" rel="external">Schedule B</a>"</cite> codes or United States Harmonized Tariff Schedule codes;</li>
<li value="2">value and terms of sale for each item (e.g. sale, loan, warranty, Incoterms 2000), including value of repairs, warranty repairs or replacement costs;</li>
<li value="3">the Contract number and financial codes (use Field 3 on the <abbr title="Canada Customs Invoice">CCI</abbr> form);</li>
<li value="4">country of origin of goods; and</li>
<li value="5">one of the following:
            <ol type="i">
<li>when a <abbr title="Canada-United States-Mexico Agreement">CUSMA</abbr> Certification of Origin/<abbr title="Canada-Israel Free Trade Agreement">CIFTA</abbr> Certificate of Origin has been prepared, a statement in the "Description" field of the <abbr title="Canada Customs Invoice">CCI</abbr> or commercial invoice confirming that the <abbr title="Canada-United States-Mexico Agreement">CUSMA</abbr> Certification of Origin/<abbr title="Canada-Israel Free Trade Agreement">CIFTA</abbr> Certificate of Origin has been completed and is attached to the <abbr title="Canada Customs Invoice">CCI</abbr> or commercial invoice; or</li>
<li>when a <abbr title="Canada-United States-Mexico Agreement">CUSMA</abbr> Certification of Origin/<abbr title="Canada-Israel Free Trade Agreement">CIFTA</abbr> Certificate of Origin was not required, a statement in the "Description" field of the <abbr title="Canada Customs Invoice">CCI</abbr> or commercial invoice certifying that the good qualifies as an originating good.</li>
</ol>
</li>
</ol>
<h2>Distribution of Documents</h2>
<ol type="1">
<li value="1">The Contractor must attach one (1) copy of the <abbr title="Canada Customs Invoice">CCI</abbr> or one (1) copy of the commercial invoice, as applicable, to shipping container No. 1 of all shipments using a waterproof envelope marked "Canada Customs Documentation".</li>
<li value="2">The second copy of each of the above-mentioned forms must be attached to the shipping documents.</li>
<li value="3">A copy of the <abbr title="Canada-Israel Free Trade Agreement">CIFTA</abbr> Certificate of Origin must be faxed to 1-800-306-1811 or emailed to
            <a href="mailto:DNDCustoms-MDNDouanes@forces.gc.ca" rel="external">DNDCustoms-MDNDouanes@forces.gc.ca</a>.</li>
</ol>
