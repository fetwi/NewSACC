<h3 class="clause-heading">Legal text for SACC item</h3><pre><div class="sacc-item">
<p>Unless otherwise stated in the Contract, the following
applies:</p>
<ol type="1">
<li value="1">For the purposes of this clause,
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">The term "<dfn>Lease</dfn>" refers to the Contract resulting
from a call-up, by which the Contractor (as Lessor) grants Canada
(as Lessee), for the term specified in the call-up, the right to
possess, use and enjoy the vehicle specified in the call-up. The
lease will be an operating and closed end type of lease, and will
not convey ownership of the vehicle to Canada nor create any
obligation on Canada to purchase the vehicle at lease expiry.</li>
<li value="2">The term "<dfn>Normal Wear and Tear</dfn>" refers to the natural
amount of deterioration, which can be expected over the term of the
lease and include:
<ol type="i">
<li value="1">tire wear, paint chips and minor scratches that do
not extend to the base metal;</li>
<li value="2">all paint scratches and paint wear and minor dents to
interior, top rails and tailgates of pick-up trucks;</li>
<li value="3">paint chips caused by stones thrown by the wheels of the
vehicles;</li>
<li value="4">frayed or stretched emergency brake cables;</li>
<li value="5">interior wear of vehicles not including holes, burns
or tears of interior surfaces;</li>
<li value="6">interior wear of trucks including all paint scratches;
and</li>
<li value="7">tire wear and damage, provided that the tires meet
provincial safety standards.</li>
</ol>
</li>
</ol>
Removal of decals or signage and any resultant paint repairs are
not considered normal wear and tear and will be chargeable as a
repair.
</li>
<li value="2">The Contractor is responsible for:
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">Delivery of the vehicle to the designated dealer
closest to the area identified in the call-up;</li>
<li value="2">Pre-servicing the vehicle in the normal way for
customer delivery;</li>
<li value="3">Supplying approximately one quarter (1/4) tank of
fuel with the vehicles delivered;</li>
<li value="4">Ensuring each vehicle delivered has the following
equipment and accessories:
<ol type="i">
<li value="1">two ignition keys</li>
<li value="2">vehicle jack</li>
<li value="3">wheel wrench</li>
<li value="4">all minimum features as detailed in the applicable
<cite>Government Motor Vehicle Ordering Guide</cite> and specifications;</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li value="5">Replacement of tires covered by the tire
manufacturer's normal warranty. (Replacement tires will be to
original equipment specifications with the same life, standard and
quality.);</li>
<li value="6">All Warranty Servicing: <dfn>"Warranty servicing"</dfn> means the
supply of parts normally provided by the manufacturer's warranty
together with the labour necessary to install such parts. The
warranty service must be made available at any dealer for the make
of vehicle leased, within Canada; and</li>
<li value="7">Inspecting the vehicle upon its return to the
designated dealer for any damages.</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li value="3">Canada is responsible for:
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">Picking up the vehicle at the designated dealer;</li>
<li value="2">Supply of fuel during the lease period;</li>
<li value="3">Oil, fluids and lubricants between and including
routine oil changes;</li>
<li value="4">Washing the vehicle;</li>
<li value="5">Maintenance in accordance with Contractor's published
maintenance schedule for the vehicle;</li>
<li value="6">Ordinary tire repairs due to road hazards and
replacement due to normal wear and tear, as required. (Replacement
tires will be to original equipment specifications with the same
life, standard and quality.);</li>
<li value="7">Providing written notification to the Contractor,
five (5) business days before the end of the lease, that the
vehicle will be returned to the closest Contractor's dealer;</li>
<li value="8">Returning the vehicle with one quarter (1/4) tank of
fuel;</li>
<li value="9">Ensuring that all original manufacturers' components
of the returned vehicle are in working condition; and</li>
<li value="10">Fines for traffic violations, including unlawful
parking issued to representatives of Canada during the lease
period.</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li value="4"><strong>Loss, damage, repairs</strong>
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">Canada is responsible for loss and damage to the
vehicle (including damage to optional equipment not requested but
accepted by Canada) during the lease period and caused or
contributed to by negligence or carelessness of representatives of
Canada and recorded to the extent that the loss or damage is not
the result of normal wear and tear. Loss or damage due to theft but
not due to negligence of Canada will be self-underwritten by
Canada.</li>
<li value="2">If a vehicle is returned to the Contractor at the end
of the lease in damaged condition, the Contractor must provide to
Canada within five (5) business days after the return of the
vehicle, a written estimate for the cost of repairs or replacement
of the loss to the authorized representative of Canada identified
in the call-up document. Repair work must be in accordance with
industry standard.</li>
<li value="3">Canada reserves the rights to obtain, through a third
party, its own estimates for the identified repairs to validate the
Contractor's estimate.</li>
<li value="4">Once the cost of repairs is agreed to by both
parties, the Contractor will invoice Canada for the agreed amount.
The Contracting Authority will resolve disagreements.</li>
<li value="5">If Canada decides to repair damage to a vehicle
during the lease period, Canada will notify the Contractor before
proceeding with the repairs. Both parties must agree to the
repairs.</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li value="5"><strong>General</strong>
<p>Where Canada requests the Contractor to plate the vehicle, an
administration charge up to a maximum of $25 may apply.</p>
</li>
</ol>
</div></pre>
