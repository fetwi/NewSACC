<h2 id="obligation-to-provide-contract-security">GC9.1 (2009-05-29) Obligation to Provide Contract Security</h2>
<ol type="1">
<li value="1">The Contractor shall, at the Contractor's expense and
within 14 days after the date that the Contractor receives notice
that the Contractor's bid was accepted by Canada, obtain and
deliver Contract Security to Canada in one of the forms prescribed
in GC9.2, "Types and Amounts of Contract Security".</li>
<li value="2">If the whole or a part of the Contract Security
provided is in the form of a security deposit, it shall be held and
disposed of in accordance with GC5.13, "Return of Security
Deposit", and GC7.4, "Security Deposit - Forfeiture or
Return".</li>
<li value="3">If a part of the Contract Security provided is in the
form of a labour and material payment bond, the Contractor shall
post a copy of that bond at the site of the Work.</li>
<li value="4">It is a condition precedent to the release of the
first progress payment that the Contractor has provided the
Contract Security as specified herein.</li>
</ol>
<h2 id="types-and-amounts-of-contract-security">GC9.2 (2014-06-26) Types and Amounts of Contract Security</h2>
<ol type="1">
<li value="1">The Contractor shall deliver to Canada either (a) or
(b).
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">A performance bond and a labour and material payment
bond each in an amount that is equal to not less than 50 percent of
the Contract Amount (excluding applicable tax(es)).</li>
<li value="2">A security deposit or an irrevocable standby letter
of credit in an amount that is equal to not less than 20 percent of
the Contract Amount (excluding applicable tax(es)).</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li value="2">A performance bond (form <a href="http://www.tpsgc-pwgsc.gc.ca/app-acq/forms/505-eng.html" rel="external">PWGSC-TPSGC 505</a>) and a labour and material payment bond (form <a href="http://www.tpsgc-pwgsc.gc.ca/app-acq/forms/506-eng.html" rel="external">PWGSC-TPSGC 506</a>) referred to in subparagraph 1)(a) of GC9.2 shall be in a
form and be issued by a bonding or surety company (see Treasury
Board Appendix L, <cite><a href="http://www.tbs-sct.gc.ca/pol/doc-eng.aspx?id=14494&amp;section=text#appL" rel="external">
Acceptable Bonding Companies</a></cite>) that is approved by Canada.</li>
<li value="3">A security deposit referred to in subparagraph 1)(b)
of GC9.2 shall be in the form of
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">a bill of exchange, bank draft or money order made
payable to the Receiver General for Canada and certified by an
approved financial institution or drawn by an approved financial
institution on itself; or</li>
<li value="2">bonds of, or unconditionally guaranteed as to
principal and interest by, the Government of Canada.</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li value="4">For the purposes of subparagraph 3)(a) of GC9.2
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">a bill of exchange is an unconditional order in
writing signed by the Contractor and addressed to an approved
financial institution, requiring the said institution to pay, on
demand, at a fixed or determinable future time a sum certain of
money to, or to the order of, the Receiver General for Canada;</li>
<li value="2">if a bill of exchange, bank draft or money order is
certified by or drawn on an institution or corporation other than a
chartered bank, it must be accompanied by proof that the said
institution or corporation meets at least one of the criteria
described in subparagraph 4)(c) of GC9.2, either by letter or by a
stamped certification on the bill of exchange, bank draft or money;
and</li>
<li value="3">An approved financial institution is
<ol type="i">
<li value="1">a corporation or institution that is a member of the
Canadian Payments Association as defined in the <cite><a href="http://laws.justice.gc.ca/eng/acts/C-21/" rel="external">Canadian Payments Act</a></cite>;</li>
<li value="2">a corporation that accepts deposits that are insured, to
the maximum permitted by law, by the Canada Deposit Insurance
Corporation or the "Autorité des marchés financiers";</li>
<li value="3">a corporation that accepts deposits from the public if
repayment of the deposit is guaranteed by Her Majesty the Queen in
right of a province;</li>
<li value="4">a corporation, association or federation incorporated or
organized as a credit union or co-operative credit society that
conforms to the requirements of a credit union which are more
particularly described in paragraph 137(6) of the <cite><a href="http://laws-lois.justice.gc.ca/eng/acts/I-3.3/index.html" rel="external">Income Tax Act</a></cite>; or</li>
<li value="5">Canada Post Corporation.</li>
</ol>
</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li value="5">Bonds referred to in subparagraph 3)(b) of GC9.2
shall be provided on the basis of their market value current at the
date of the Contract, and shall be
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">made payable to bearer; or</li>
<li value="2">accompanied by a duly executed instrument of transfer
of the bonds to the Receiver General for Canada in the form
prescribed by the Domestic Bonds of Canada Regulations; or</li>
<li value="3">registered as to principal, or as to principal and
interest, in the name of the Receiver General for Canada pursuant
to the Domestic Bonds of Canada Regulations.</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li value="6">An irrevocable standby letter of credit referred to
in subparagraph 1)(b) of GC9.2 shall
<ol type="a">
<li value="1">be an arrangement, however named or described,
whereby a financial institution (the "Issuer") acting at the
request and on the instructions of a customer (the "Applicant") or
on its own behalf,
<ol type="i">
<li value="1">is to make a payment to, or to the order of, Canada
as the beneficiary;</li>
<li value="2">is to accept and pay bills of exchange drawn by Canada;</li>
<li value="3">authorizes another financial institution to effect such
payment or accept and pay such bills of exchange; or</li>
<li value="4">authorizes another financial institution to negotiate
against written demand(s) for payment provided that the terms and
conditions of the letter of credit are complied with;</li>
</ol>
</li>
<li value="2">state the face amount that may be drawn against
it;</li>
<li value="3">state its expiry date;</li>
<li value="4">provide for sight payment to the Receiver General for
Canada by way of the financial institution's draft against
presentation of a written demand for payment signed by Canada;</li>
<li value="5">provide that more than one written demand for payment
may be presented subject to the sum of those demands not exceeding
the face value of the letter of credit;</li>
<li value="6">provide that it is subject to the International
Chamber of Commerce (ICC) Uniform Customs and Practice (UCP) for
Documentary Credits, 2007 Revision, <abbr title="International Chamber of Commerce">ICC</abbr> Publication <abbr title="Number">No.</abbr> 600.
Pursuant to the <abbr title="International Chamber of Commerce">ICC</abbr> <abbr title="Uniform Customs and Practice">UCP</abbr>, a credit is irrevocable even if there is
no indication to that effect; and</li>
<li value="7">be issued or confirmed, in either official language
in a format left to the discretion of the issuer or confirmer, by
an approved financial institution on its letterhead.</li>
</ol>
</li>
</ol>
