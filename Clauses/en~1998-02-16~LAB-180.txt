<strong>LAB-180       01     (01/05/96)    Interpretation</strong>

1.     In these conditions,

       <strong>"Act"</strong> means the <em>Fair Wages and Hours of Labour Act</em>;

       <strong>"Contract"</strong> means the Contract to which these Labour Conditions are 
       attached;

       <strong>"Contracting Authority"</strong> means the department of Government or 
       Corporation that is an agent of Canada with whom the contract is 
       made;

       <strong>"Contractor"</strong> means the person who has entered into the contract with 
       the Contracting Authority;

       <strong>"Director"</strong> means a regional director of Human Resources Development 
       Canada, Labour Program in Moncton, Montr√©al, Ottawa, Toronto, 
       Winnipeg and Vancouver;

       <strong>"Fair Wage Officer"</strong> means the officer of Human Resources Development 
       Canada, Labour Program designated by the Minister;

       <strong>"Minister"</strong> means the Canada Minister of Employment and Immigration;

       <strong>"Persons</strong>" means those workers employed by the Contractor, 
       Subcontractor or any other person doing or contracting to do the 
       whole or any part of the work contemplated by the Contract;

       <strong>"Regulations"</strong> means the <em>Fair Wages and Hours of Labour Regulations</em> 
       made pursuant to the Act;

       <strong>"Road, Sewer and Watermain Construction"</strong> means clearing and 
       preparing a right of way, excavation and subgrading, laying a 
       granular base, grading and asphalt and concrete paving and includes:

       (a)    the operation of on-site plants to service such construction;
       (b)    the installation of drainage;
       (c)    landscaping;
       (d)    the demolition of structures within or affected by a right of 
              way; and
       (e)    all other work involved in:

              (1)    the construction, reconstruction and maintenance of 
                     roads, highways, streets, sidewalks, curbs, parking 
                     lots, driveways, airport runways, airport taxi strips 
                     and aircraft parking aprons; and
 
              (2)    the installation, reinstallation and maintenance of 
                     sewers and watermains.


<strong>LAB-180       02     (01/05/96)    General Fair Wage Clause</strong>

1.     All persons in the employ of the Contractor, Subcontractor, and/or 
       other person doing or contracting to do the whole or any part of the 
       Work contemplated by the Contract shall, during the continuance of 
       the Work, be paid fair wages; that is, such wages as are generally 
       accepted as current for competent workers in the district in which 
       the Work is being performed for the character or class of work in 
       which such workers are respectively engaged; and in no case shall 
       the wage rates paid be less than the minimum hourly rate of pay 
       prescribed by or pursuant to Part III of the Canada Labour Code 
       (Labour Standards).

2.     Where, during the term of the Contract, a question arises as to the 
       fair wages for a particular character or class of work, the Director 
       may determine the wage rates that are to be considered as fair wages 
       for the purpose of the Contract; and in making his determination, 
       the Director may specify the effective date of the fair wages which 
       he has ascertained, and the Contractor agrees that where, during the 
       term of the Contract, he receives from the Contracting Authority a 
       copy of any determination of fair wage rates referred to above, the 
       Contractor will pay the employees concerned a wage rate which is not 
       less than the wage rate determined above; such wage rate to be 
       retroactive to the specified effective date.

3.     The Contractor agrees that where, during the term of the Contract 
       any question arises as to the proper classification of employment of 
       work for the purpose of the payment of fair wages, the 
       classification shall be determined by the Director.


<strong>LAB-180       03     (01/05/96)    Hours of Work</strong>

1.     Except as provided in subsection 03(4) and section 13, the working 
       hours of persons employed in the execution of the Contract shall not 
       exceed 8 hours in a day or 48 hours in a week except where longer 
       daily or weekly hours are authorized by the Minister in cases of 
       exceptional circumstances.

2.     Except as provided in Section 13, all persons shall be paid for 
       hours worked in excess of 8 hours in a day or 40 hours in a week at 
       an overtime rate at least one and one-half times the wage rates 
       required to be paid under these Labour Conditions, as set out in 
       subsection 02(1).
 
3.     Except as provided in section 13, all applications for permission to 
       exceed 8 hours in a day or 48 hours in a week shall be made to the 
       Contracting Authority for reference to the Minister.

4.     The daily or weekly hours of work set out in subsection 03(1) or 
       section 13, or authorized by the Minister under subsection 03(1) or 
       section 13, may be exceeded where there is serious interference with 
       the ordinary carrying out of the Work contemplated by the Contract 
       by reason of:

       (a)    an accident involving injury to a worker;

       (b)    a breakdown of or damage to machinery or equipment; or

       (c)    other unforeseen or unpreventable circumstances, but only to 
              the extent necessary to prevent such serious interference.

5.     Where, because of serious interference referred to in subsection 03(
       4), the working hours of any worker exceeded the weekly hours of 
       work set out in subsection 03(1) or Section 13, or authorized by the 
       Minister under subsection 03(1) or Section 13, the Contractor shall 
       report in writing to the Director, within fifteen (15) days after 
       the end of the week in which such hours of work were exceeded, 
       setting forth:

       (a)    the nature of the serious interference;

       (b)    the names of all workers who worked in excess of weekly hours 
              of work set out in subsection 03(1) or section 13 or 
              authorized by the Minister under subsection 03(1) or section 
              13; and

       (c)    the number of hours each worker worked in excess of the 
              weekly hours of work referred to in paragraph (b).


<strong>LAB-180       04     (01/05/96)    Labour Conditions to be Posted</strong>

For the protection of all persons, the Contractor agrees to post and keep 
posted, in a conspicuous place on the premises where work contemplated by 
the Contract is being carried out or on premises occupied or used by 
persons engaged in carrying out such work, a copy of these Labour 
Conditions, and any authorization by the Minister to exceed the maximum 
hours specified in subsection 03(1) or paragraph 13(a).


<strong>LAB-180       05     (01/05/96)    The Contractor to Keep Records which are 
                                          to be Kept Open</strong>
 
1.     The Contractor agrees to keep proper books and records showing the 
       names, addresses and classifications of employment and work of all 
       workers employed in work under the Contract and the rate of wages, 
       the wages paid and the daily hours worked by such workers.

2.     The Contractor also agrees to undertake that his books, records and 
       premises will be open at all times for inspection by the Minister, a 
       Fair Wage Officer or any other person designated by the Minister.

3.     The Contractor also agrees to furnish the Minister, the Contracting 
       Authority, the Fair Wage Officer or other person designated by the 
       Minister, on request, with such further information as is required 
       to ascertain that the requirements of the Act, the Regulations and 
       the Contract with respect to wages, hours of work and other labour 
       conditions have been complied with.


<strong>LAB-180       06     (01/05/96)    Departmental Requirements before Payment 
                                          made to Contractor</strong>

1.     The Contractor agrees that he will not be entitled to payment of any 
       money otherwise payable under the Contract until he has filed with 
       the Contracting Authority in support of his claim for payment a 
       sworn statement:

       (a)    that he has kept the books and records required by these 
              Regulations;

       (b)    as to whether any wages in respect of work performed under 
              the Contract remain in arrears; and

       (c)    that to his knowledge, all the conditions in the Contract 
              required by the Act and the Regulations have been complied 
              with.

2.     The Contractor also agrees that, where the Minister informs the 
       Contracting Authority and the Contractor that fair wages and 
       overtime rates have not been paid by the Contractor to persons 
       employed under the Contract, the Contracting Authority will be 
       entitled to withhold from any monies otherwise payable under the 
       Contract the amount the Minister indicates should be withheld until 
       such time as the matter has been dealt with to the satisfaction of 
       the Minister.


<strong>LAB-180       07     (01/05/96)    Authority to pay Wages in the Event of 
                                          Default by the Contractor</strong>

1.     The Contractor agrees that where he is in default of payment of 
       wages to an employee, the Contractor will send to the Minister a 
       cheque payable to the Receiver General for Canada for the amount 
       determined by the Minister as being the amount the Contractor is in 
       default.

2.     The Contractor agrees that where he fails to comply with subsection 
       07(1), the Minister may instruct the Contracting Authority to pay to 
       the Receiver General for Canada, out of any monies otherwise payable 
       to the Contractor, the amount determined by the Minister as being 
       the amount the Contractor is in default.


<strong>LAB-180       08     (01/05/96)    Conditions of Subcontracting</strong>

The Contractor agrees that in subcontracting any part of the Work 
contemplated by the Contract, the Contractor will place in the Subcontract 
the conditions respecting wages, hours of work and other labour conditions 
set out in the Contract.  The Contractor further agrees that the Contractor 
will be responsible for carrying out of the conditions.


<strong>LAB-180       09     (01/05/96)    Workers to be Residents of Canada</strong>

1.     The Contractor agrees that all persons employed by the Contractor to 
       do any work under the Contract will be residents of Canada unless, 
       in the opinion of the Contracting Authority at the time the Work is 
       to commence under the Contract:

       (a)    persons resident in Canada are not available to do the Work; 
              or

       (b)    special circumstances exist whereby it would not be in the 
              public interest to require that all such persons be residents 
              of Canada.


<strong>LAB-180       10     (01/05/96)    Non-discrimination in Hiring and 
                                          Employment of Labour</strong>

1.     The Contractor agrees that:

       (a)    in the hiring and employment of workers to perform any work 
              under the Contract, the Contractor will not refuse to employ 
              and will not discriminate in any manner against any person 
              because:

              (1)    of that person's race, national origin, colour, 
                     religion, age, sex or marital status;

              (2)    of the race, national origin, colour, religion, age, 
                     sex, or marital status of any person having any 
                     relationship or association with that person; or 
              (3)    a complaint has been made or information has been 
                     given by or in respect of that person relating to an 
                     alleged failure by the Contractor to comply with 
                     subparagraph (1) or (2);

       (b)    if any question arises as to whether the Contractor has 
              failed to comply with the provision described in paragraph (a), 
              the Minister or any person designated by the Minister shall 
              decide the question and his decision shall be final for the 
              purposes of the contract; and

       (c)    failure to comply with the aforementioned paragraphs (a) and 
              (b) regarding non-discrimination shall constitute a material 
              breach of the contract.


<strong>LAB-180       11     (01/05/96)    Liquidated Damages</strong>

In the event of any default in carrying out any of the conditions set out 
in sections 02, 03 and 13 of these Labour Conditions in respect of any 
employee, the Contractor shall pay to Canada as liquidated damages a sum of $50 
for every such default, and the Minister under whom the Work contemplated 
by the Contract is being executed may direct that the amount assessed as 
liquidated damages under this section be deducted from any moneys payable 
to the Contractor under the Contract and be credited to the Consolidated 
Revenue Fund.


<strong>LAB-180       12     (01/05/96)    Schedule of Wage Rates</strong>

A schedule of minimum wage rates, authorized by Human Resources Development 
Canada, Labour Program, when attached to these Labour Conditions as 
Appendix A also becomes part of the Contract.


<strong>LAB-180       13     (01/05/96)    Contracts exclusively for Road, Sewer 
                                          and Watermain Construction</strong>

1.     In a Contract which is EXCLUSIVELY for road, sewer and watermain 
       construction:

       (a)    The working hours of persons shall not exceed 60 hours in a 
              week except where longer weekly hours are authorized by the 
              Minister under the Act.

       (b)    All persons shall be paid for hours worked in excess of 10 
              hours in a day or 50 hours in a week at an overtime rate at 
              least one and one-half times the wage rates required to be 
              paid under these Labour Conditions, as set out in subsection 
              02(1).

       (c)    An application for authorization by the Minister to exceed 
              the working hours specified in paragraph (a) shall be 
              forwarded to him through the contracting authority.


<strong>NOTICE</strong>

<strong>Contractors should be aware that if Human Resources Development Canada, 
Labour Program is required to establish a wage rate during the term of this 
Contract for the purpose of the payment of "Fair Wages", such rate will 
ordinarily be drawn from the following sources:</strong>

       (a)    in the province of Quebec, rates established by that province 
              for the purposes of the Quebec "<em>Construction Decree</em>";

       (b)    in the province of Manitoba, rates established by that 
              province for the purposes of the Manitoba "<em>Construction 
              Industry Wages Act</em>";

       (c)    in the province of Ontario, rates established by that 
              province for the purposes of the Ontario "<em>Government 
              Contracts Hours and Wages Act</em>";

       (d)    in the province of New Brunswick, rates established by that 
              province for the purposes of the "<em>Minimum Wage for Categories 
              of Employees in Crown Construction Work Regulation</em>" under the 
              "<em>New Brunswick Employment Standards Act</em>";

       (e)    in the Yukon Territory, rates established by that territory 
              for the purposes of the Fair Wage Regulation under the Yukon 
              Territory "<em>Employment Standards Act</em>";

       (f)    in the provinces of Newfoundland, Nova Scotia, Prince Edward 
              Island, Saskatchewan, Alberta and British Columbia and in the 
              Northwest Territories, rates will be determined through 
              surveys of local labour markets.
