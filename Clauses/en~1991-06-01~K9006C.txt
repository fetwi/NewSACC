   Production Act, R.S.C. 1985 c.D-1.  In accordance with the provisions of 
   section 20 of that Act, title to any government issue (as defined in 
   that Act) furnished or made available to the Contractor or obtained or 
   constructed by it with money provided by Her Majesty or Her agents 
   remains vested or vests in Her Majesty free and clear of all claims, 
   liens, charges and encumbrances. Notwithstanding any law in force in any 
   province or any territory of Canada but subject to the provisions of the 
   Contract, Her Majesty is entitled, at any time, to remove, sell or 
   dispose of the government issue.  Without restricting the generality of 
   the foregoing, in the event of default by the Contractor, Her Majesty 
   shall have the immediate right to enter the shipyard, without first 
   obtaining a court order, and to take possession of the "Vessel" and all 
   other property of Her Majesty, including, but not limited to, work in 
   process located on the premises, and to perform any further work 
   required to enable the "Vessel", and other such property, to be removed 
   from the shipyard.
