<strong>1053   01     (16/02/98)    Interpretation</strong>

1.     In the <em>Contract</em>,

       "<strong><em>Agreement</em></strong>" means the document, signed by the parties in each case, 
       that refers to these general conditions;

       "<strong><em>Canada</em></strong>", "<strong>Crown</strong>", or "<strong>Her Majesty</strong>" mean Her Majesty the Queen in 
       right of Canada;

       "<strong><em>Contract</em></strong>" includes the <em>Agreement</em>, these general conditions and 
       every other document referred to in the <em>Agreement</em> as forming part of 
       the <em>Contract</em>;

       "<strong><em>Government Issue</em></strong>" includes everything that may be furnished to the 
       Contractor by or on behalf of <em>Canada</em> for the purposes of the <em>
       Contract</em>;

       "<strong><em>invention</em></strong>" means any new and useful art, process, machine, 
       manufacture or composition of matter, or any new and useful 
       improvement thereof;

       "<strong><em>Minister</em></strong>" means the Minister of Public Works and Government 
       Services and any other person authorized to act on his behalf;

       "<strong><em>Work</em></strong>" means everything required to be furnished or performed by the 
       Contractor under the <em>Contract</em>;

2.     Words importing male persons include female persons and corporations;

3.     Words in the singular include the plural and words in the plural 
       include the singular.

4.     Where there is a conflict, the provisions of the <em>Agreement</em> shall 
       prevail over the provision of these general conditions.


<strong>1053   02     (01/06/91)    Powers of Minister</strong>

The <em>Minister</em> is the agent of <em>Canada</em> for all purposes of the Contract.  
Nothing contained in or omitted from the <em>Contract</em> shall restrict any right 
or power of <em>Canada</em> or the <em>Minister</em> existing under any act of the Parliament 
of Canada or otherwise.  Every right or power of the <em>Minister</em> under the <em>
Contract</em> or otherwise shall be cumulative and non-exclusive.


<strong>1053   03     (01/06/91)    Assignment and Subcontracting</strong>

1.     The Contractor may not assign the <em>Contract</em> without the prior written 
       consent of the <em>Minister</em>. 
2.     The Contractor may subcontract any portion of the <em>Work</em> as is 
       customary in performing similar contracts but the Contractor may not 
       subcontract any other portion of the <em>Work</em> without the prior written 
       consent of the <em>Minister</em>.

3.     No assignment or subcontract relieves the Contractor from any 
       obligation under the <em>Contract</em> or impose any liability upon <em>Canada</em> or 
       the <em>Minister</em> to an assignee or subcontractor.

4.     Subject to the preceding provisions of this section, the <em>Contract</em> 
       shall have effect for the benefit of and shall be binding upon the 
       successors and assigns of <em>Canada</em> and of the Contractor.


<strong>1053   04     (01/06/91)    Conduct of Work</strong>

1.     The Contractor shall,

       (a)    perform the <em>Work</em> promptly;

       (b)    supervise the <em>Work</em> to ensure that it is performed efficiently, 
              in conformity with the requirements of the <em>Contract </em>and in 
              accordance with the highest standards of professional quality; 
              and 

       (c)    use only competent personnel to perform the <em>Work</em>.

2.     The <em>Minister</em> shall have access to the <em>Work</em> at all reasonable times.


<strong>1053   05     (16/02/98)    Crown Property</strong>

1.     <em>Government Issue</em> may be used by the Contractor only for the purposes 
       of the <em>Contract</em> and remains vested in <em>Canada</em>.  The Contractor shall 
       maintain a record of all <em>Government Issue</em> and, where practicable, 
       mark all items of <em>Government Issue</em> as being the property of <em>Canada</em>.

2.     Except for ordinary wear and tear, any government issue not 
       incorporated into the <em>Work</em> shall be returned to <em>Canada</em> upon demand 
       in the same condition as when furnished to the Contractor.

3.     The Contractor shall take reasonable and proper care of any property 
       vested in <em>Canada</em> while such property is in the possession of the 
       Contractor or subject to his control and he shall be responsible for 
       any loss or damage resulting from his failure to do so other than 
       loss or damage caused by ordinary wear and tear.


<strong>1053   06     (01/06/91)    Publication Rights</strong> 
1.     In this section,

       (a)    "copyright work" means any work in which a copyright may 
              subsist, produced in or as a result of performing the <em>
              Contract</em>;

       (b)    "publication" or "publish" do not include disclosure to an 
              academic supervisor or appraiser, for the sole purpose of 
              academic evaluation.

2.     Copyright in any copyright work vests in <em>Canada</em> but, in any 
       publication of such work by or on behalf of <em>Canada</em>, the contribution 
       of the Contractor and of the author shall be acknowledged.

3.     The Contractor and the author each shall have a royalty-free 
       non-exclusive license to publish or have published any copyright 
       work in the course of the normal dissemination of knowledge in the 
       subject field, but they shall not publish or have published any 
       copyright work during the performance of the contract or for a 
       period of three (3) months thereafter without the prior written 
       consent of the <em>Minister</em>.

4.     Any copyright work published by or on behalf of the Contractor or 
       the author shall acknowledge that the Work was performed under <em>
       Contract</em> with <em>Canada</em>, unless the <em>Minister</em> gives notice to the 
       contrary.


<strong>1053   07     (01/06/91)    Ownership of Patents and Use of Technical 
                                   Information</strong>

1.     Any technical report, photograph, drawing, plan, specification, 
       model, prototype, pattern or sample produced by the Contractor in 
       performing the <em>Contract</em> shall vest in <em>Canada</em>.  The Contractor shall 
       keep a record of everything mentioned in this subsection and account 
       to the <em>Minister</em> therefor.

2.     Any design, technical information, invention, method or process 
       conceived or developed or first actually reduced to practice in 
       performing the <em>Contract</em> shall vest in <em>Canada</em>.  The Contractor shall 
       fully and promptly disclose in writing to the <em>Minister</em> anything 
       mentioned in this subsection.

3.     Notwithstanding the provisions of section 06, the Contractor shall 
       not, except as it is necessary to perform the <em>Contract</em>, divulge or 
       publish any information in respect of anything mentioned in 
       subsection 2 of this section for a period of twelve (12) months 
       after disclosure shall have been made to the <em>Minister</em> in accordance 
       with that subsection.
 
4.     The Contractor shall have a royalty-free non-exclusive license to 
       use and exercise and have used and exercised any invention mentioned 
       in subsection 2 of this section for the academic and research 
       purposes of the Contractor.


<strong>1053   08     (01/06/91)    Title on Progress Payments</strong>

1.     Upon any payment made to the Contractor, whether it is made as a 
       progress payment, accountable advance or otherwise, in respect of 
       the <em>Work</em> or any portion of the <em>Work</em>, title to the <em>Work</em> or to any 
       portion in respect of which payment is made shall vest in <em>Canada</em>.

2.     Any vesting of title mentioned in subsection 1 shall not constitute 
       acceptance by <em>Canada</em> of the <em>Work</em> or any portion of the <em>Work</em> and 
       shall not relieve the Contractor of any obligation to perform the 
       work in accordance with the <em>Contract</em>.


<strong>1053   09     (01/06/91)    Termination, Suspension, Change</strong>

1.     The <em>Minister</em> may, by giving notice to the Contractor, terminate, 
       suspend or reduce the scope of the <em>Work</em> or any portion of the <em>Work</em>.  
       The Contractor shall immediately comply with the requirements of any 
       such notice and in so doing take every reasonable action that will 
       minimize the cost of performing the <em>Contract</em> and complying with the 
       notice.

2.     When, as a result of a notice mentioned in subsection 1,

       (a)    any suspension or reduction in the scope of the work results 
              in an increase or decrease in the cost of performing the <em>
              Contract</em>, the price of the <em>Contract</em> shall be adjusted 
              accordingly; and

       (b)    the <em>Work</em> or any portion of the <em>Work</em> is terminated, the 
              Contractor shall be paid the reasonable cost of performing 
              any of the work that has been performed at the time of 
              termination and any other cost directly and necessarily 
              incurred as a result of the termination.

3.     Where, as a direct result of the loss of any person having a unique 
       capability in relation to the <em>Work</em>, the Contractor is of the opinion 
       that the <em>Contract</em> cannot be performed, he shall immediately give 
       notice to the Minister of these circumstances and if the <em>Minister</em> is 
       of the opinion that the <em>Contract</em> cannot be performed and that such 
       loss was beyond the control of the Contractor, he shall terminate 
       the work in the manner mentioned in subsection 1.

 
<strong>1053   10     (16/02/98)    Notice</strong>

1.     Where in the <em>Contract</em> any notice is required to be given, it shall 
       be sent by registered mail or by telegram addressed to the party for 
       whom it is intended at the address mentioned in the <em>Contract</em>, and if 
       no address is mentioned in the <em>Contract</em>,

       (a)    in the case of the Contractor, addressed to his latest known 
              address; or

       (b)    in the case of the <em>Minister</em>, addressed to the Headquarters of 
              the Department of Public Works and Government Services,

       and any notice shall be deemed to have been given when it is sent.


<strong>1053   11     (01/06/91)    Accounts</strong>

1.     The Contractor shall,

       (a)    keep accounts and records of the cost of performing the <em>
              Contract</em> and keep all documents relating to such cost and, 
              unless he obtains the prior written consent of the <em>Minister</em> 
              to otherwise dispose of such accounts, records and documents, 
              preserve them for a period of six (6) years from the end of 
              the calendar year in which the <em>Contract</em> is terminated or 
              completed; and

       (b)    on demand, produce to the <em>Minister</em> every account, record or 
              document mentioned in paragraph 1.(a) that may be required by 
              him and permit the <em>Minister</em> to examine, audit and take copies 
              and extracts from such accounts, records or documents.


<strong>1053   12     (01/06/91)    Indemnity against Claims</strong>

1.     The Contractor shall indemnify and save harmless <em>Canada</em> in respect 
       of any claim, loss, damage or expense relating to,

       (a)    any injury or death of a person, or loss of or damage to 
              property, caused or alleged to be caused as a result of 
              performing the <em>Contract</em>; and

       (b)    any lien, attachment, charge, encumbrance or similar claim 
              upon any property vested in <em>Canada</em> under the <em>Contract</em>; and

       (c)    any use or infringement of patented invention or copyright in 
              performing the <em>Contract</em> or as a result of the use of the work 
              by <em>Canada</em>. 

<strong>1053   13     (01/06/91)    Further Assurances</strong>

Where title to any property of any description vests in <em>Canada</em> under the <em>
Contract</em>, the Contractor shall execute such conveyances and other documents 
relating to title as the <em>Minister</em> may require to evidence the title of <em>
Canada</em>.


<strong>1053   14     (01/06/94)    No Bribe, Etc.</strong>

The Contractor warrants that no bribe, gift, or other inducement has been 
paid, given, promised or offered to any official or employee of <em>Canada</em> for, 
or with a view to, the obtaining of the <em>Contract</em> by the Contractor.


<strong>1053   15     (01/06/91)    Labour and Health Conditions</strong>

The Contractor shall comply with all labour conditions, and with all health 
conditions and requirements, from time to time applicable to the <em>Work</em>.


<strong>1053   16     (01/06/91)    Members of the House of Commons</strong>

No member of the House of Commons shall be admitted to any share or part of 
the <em>Contract</em> or to any benefit to arise therefrom.


<strong>1053   17     (16/02/98)    Security and Protection of the Work</strong>

1.     When the <em>Contract</em>, the <em>Work</em>, or any information provided to the 
       Contractor by or on behalf of <em>Canada</em> in connection with the <em>Work</em>, or 
       any information developed by the Contractor as part of the <em>Work</em>, is 
       identified as TOP SECRET, SECRET, CONFIDENTIAL or PROTECTED by <em>
       Canada</em>, the Contractor shall, at all times, take all measures 
       reasonably necessary for the safeguarding of the material so 
       identified, including those set out in the Department of Public 
       Works and Government Services <em>Industrial Security Manual </em>and its 
       supplements and any other instructions issued by the <em>Minister</em>.  
       Where this subsection applies, notwithstanding sections 06 and 07, 
       the Contractor shall keep confidential the material so identified, 
       and shall not disclose any such material to any person without the 
       written permission of the <em>Minister</em>, except that the Contractor may 
       disclose to a subcontractor, authorized in accordance with this <em>
       Contract</em>, information necessary to the performance of the 
       subcontract.

2.     Without limiting the generality of subsection 1, when the <em>Contract</em>, 
       the <em>Work</em>, or any information referred to in subsection 1 is 
       identified as TOP SECRET, SECRET, CONFIDENTIAL or PROTECTED by <em>
       Canada</em>, the <em>Minister</em> shall be entitled to inspect the Contractor's 
       premises and the premises of a subcontractor at any tier for 
       security purposes at any time during the term of the <em>Contract</em>, and 
       the Contractor shall comply with, and ensure that any such 
       subcontractor complies with, all written instructions issued by the <em>
       Minister</em> dealing with the material so identified, including any 
       requirement that employees of the Contractor or of any such 
       subcontractor execute and deliver declarations relating to 
       reliability screenings, security clearances and other procedures.


<strong>1053   18     (06/06/94)    Certification - Contingency Fees</strong>

1.     The Contractor certifies that it has not directly or indirectly paid 
       or agreed to pay and covenants that it will not directly or 
       indirectly pay a contingency fee for the solicitation, negotiation 
       or obtaining of this <em>Contract</em> to any person other than an employee 
       acting in the normal course of the employee's duties.

2.     All accounts and records pertaining to payments of fees or other 
       compensation for the solicitation, obtaining or negotiation of the <em>
       Contract</em> shall be subject to the Accounts and Audit provisions of 
       the <em>Contract</em>.

3.     If the Contractor certifies falsely under this section or is in 
       default of the obligations contained therein, the <em>Minister</em> may 
       either terminate this <em>Contract</em> for default in accordance with the 
       termination for default provisions of the <em>Contract</em> or recover from 
       the Contractor by way of reduction to the Contract Price or 
       otherwise the full amount of the contingency fee.

4.     In this section,

       "<strong>contingency fe</strong>e" means any payment or other compensation that is 
       contingent upon or is calculated upon the basis of a degree of 
       success in soliciting or obtaining a government <em>Contract</em> or 
       negotiating the whole or any part of its terms;

       "<strong>employee</strong>" means a person with whom the Contractor has an employer/
       employee relationship;
       
       "<strong>person</strong>" includes an individual or group of individuals, a 
       corporation, a partnership, an organization and an association and, 
       without restricting the generality of the foregoing, includes any 
       individual who is required to file a return with the registrar 
       pursuant to section 5 of the <em>Lobbyist Registration Act</em> R.S. 1985 c.44 
       (4th Supplement) as the same may be amended from time to time.
